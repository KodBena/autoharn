# ADR-0014-style consultation: semantics for violations whose target row is retracted (the s37 eternal-debt defect)

Fresh-context Fable instance, 2026-07-16. Inputs: the witnessed problem statement; `kernel/lineage/s37-violation-disposition.sql` and `s31-supersession-uniform-retraction.sql` (worktree `agent-a3af0fe41aff93db2`); `design/FABLE-ORPHAN-DISPOSITION-SPEC.md` v2; `gates/ledger_reader_allowlist.py`; `law/briefs/safety-critical-logging/BRIEF.md` §2; ADR-0000, ADR-0012 (structure), ADR-0013 (rule headings); published literature per part 3. No commissioner analysis received, per ADR-0018.

## 1. Plain-language answer

**The semantics should be: retracting the defective row is itself the answer to the violation.** When an operator supersedes the row a violation complains about — the dangling dependency edge, the witness-less close — that violation should drop out of the debt view automatically, in the same read, with no disposition needed. The full story (the defect, the retraction that settled it, any dispositions along the way) stays permanently visible in `work_violation_history`. Dispositions remain the answering act for the other case: a defect whose target row is still standing.

The one-paragraph why. Your ledger has two kinds of question, and the house already knows this — the s31 gate forces every reader to declare itself either a *current-truth* reader ("what is the state now") or a *history* reader ("what was ever written"). Before s37, `work_item_violations` was honestly a history reader: a registry of every defect ever written, and its raw-history arms were correct for that job. s37 changed what the view *means* — it became the debt register, the thing the Stop hook blocks on, where non-membership means "answered" — and debt is a current-truth question. But two of its ingredients kept the old history typing: the row-targeted arms still quantify over retracted rows, and (the directly witnessed defect) the basis check demands the target row still be in `ledger_current`. Under s31, retraction is reinstatement-free — a retracted row is retracted forever — so "target retracted" is a *permanent* condition, and a basis check that reads it as "basis lapsed" manufactures permanent, unanswerable debt. That is a category error, not a tuning problem: the target's retraction can never *defeat* a retirement basis, because nothing the retraction removed can ever come back to re-create the defect; retraction can only settle a defect further. In your Haskell terms: debt must be a fold over the in-force events, but the current code computes membership over the raw stream while checking answers against the folded one — two different worlds quantified in one predicate. Pick one world (the in-force one, because debt is about *now*), and the trap is not patched but unrepresentable.

## 2. Recommendation in spec-amendment-ready terms

Semantics only; no SQL. All within s31's reinstatement-free retraction, the s37 validity-bounds principle, BRIEF I3/I4/I7, and ADR-0000.

**A. Name the target typing the delta already carries implicitly.** Every violations member has a `target_id`. Classes split by what that target *is*: **row-targeted** (the defect inheres in one ledger row: `shipped_without_witness`, `depends_on_unknown_slug`, `dangling_parent`, the four `orphaned_by_retraction` sub-cases, `closed_but_tree_defeated`) and **slug-targeted** (the defect inheres in a configuration; the target is the slug's `work_opened` row used as a handle: `duplicate_open`, `dependency_cycle`, `parent_cycle`, `blocks_close_cycle`).

**B. The debt-membership amendment (the core).** A violations member is DEBT only while its target row is in force. A row-targeted member whose target row has been retracted lapses from `work_item_violations` in the same read — the retraction row is its answering act, carrying the same uniform fold-removal meaning s31 gives retraction everywhere else. A slug-targeted member whose handle row is retracted lapses likewise (the slug has left current truth; the surviving children are exactly what the `orphaned_by_retraction` members exist to surface, and those are already current-truth arms). One sentence for the spec: **the debt projection quantifies over in-force rows only; the record projection quantifies over everything, forever.**

**C. The record is never thinner (I4).** `work_violation_history` keeps its raw, unfiltered arms unchanged, and should additionally surface, per lapsed member, the superseding row that answered it — so "what became of this defect?" always has an attributable answer on the record: a disposition, a retraction, or open debt. Nothing else in history semantics changes.

**D. Disposition validity bounds, corrected to their spirit.** Remove the unconditional requirement that the disposition's target row be in `ledger_current`. A basis lapses only when a *named, genuinely resurrectable* condition re-fires — for a `retired` disposition on a settled child, the child's close being superseded (the spec's own A4 example, which stays fully live under this amendment, since a work item is resurrectable even though a row is not); for `reissued`, the cited successor leaving force (unchanged). A disposition whose target row is later retracted becomes *moot* (its member already lapsed), never *defeated*. I7 is thereby kept where it can actually fire and stripped where it was vacuously inverted: a validity bound conditioned on an impossible event (un-retraction) is not a bound, and s37 had accidentally coded it as its own negation.

**E. Closure (ADR-0000 Rule 2(a) form).** Invariant: every `work_item_violations` member has, at every moment, at least one reachable answering act — a disposition while its target is in force, the target's own retraction otherwise; the state "debt with no possible answering act" is unrepresentable because the debt predicate and the answer predicate quantify over the same world (`ledger_current`). Quantification universe: all eleven member classes, both target typings — note the witnessed specimens (`depends_on_unknown_slug`, plus the panel's orphan case) do NOT exhaust the class; by the same failing join, every slug-targeted class (`duplicate_open`, `dependency_cycle`, `parent_cycle`, `blocks_close_cycle`) and every other row-targeted class had the identical reachable trap the moment its target row is superseded. Any future violations member must declare its target typing and, if it names a resurrectable basis condition, name it explicitly. Denomination: debt in in-force row ids; record in raw row ids.

**F. What does NOT change.** The write-boundary triggers' raw reads (slug burn, duplicate-open refusal — ratified fork 2) are untouched: fork 2 governs write-side identity, not debt accounting; a burned slug stays burned. No auto-written disposition rows (derived, never stored). No kind-compatibility refusal on `supersedes` (fork 1 stands). The engine twin (`work_items.lp` / `ledger_floor.py`) mirrors the same membership rule, `./judge` AGREE on both polarities, per the s37 spec's own Element 2 discipline. `gates/ledger_reader_allowlist.py`'s entry for `work_item_violations` retypes accordingly: its debt arms become current-truth-factored; the raw structural-existence reads live wholly in `work_violation_history`'s existing declared-history entry.

**Alternatives considered and rejected.** (1) *Let dispositions answer retracted targets* (point the basis join at raw history): keeps debt history-typed, makes I7 permanently vacuous for those rows, and — worse — turns every routine, CLI-endorsed supersede repair into a two-act ceremony (retract, then also disposition "retired: I retracted it"), exactly the ritual-rather-than-load-bearing paperwork the 2026-07-11 ruling deletes. (2) *Auto-disposition on supersede*: violates the spec's "does not auto-disposition anything" and the house derived-never-stored posture. (3) *Refuse superseding a violation's target*: a kind/role-compatibility refusal on `supersedes`, which ratified fork 1 explicitly declined, and it would block the repair the CLI itself recommends.

## 3. Literature grounding

The maintainer's instinct was right: this is a known problem, and it is owned jointly by two fields — temporal databases (which own the *diagnosis*) and deontic logic / normative systems (which own the *debt semantics*). Consulted this session via web search unless marked otherwise.

**Bears directly — temporal databases.** Transaction-time relations are append-only; a correction is a new assertion, and the old one is retained for audit — precisely your ledger. The literature's central discipline is that every query is *typed by timeline*: current ("as of now"), sequenced, or non-sequenced over the full assertion history — and a query must commit to one. [Snodgrass's temporal-database corpus](https://www2.cs.arizona.edu/~rts/pubs/SIGMODRecordDec90.pdf) and the [TSQL2 data model](https://people.cs.aau.dk/~csj/Thesis/pdf/chapter12.pdf) (Jensen & Snodgrass) set this out, including worked correction examples; [SQL:2011's system-versioned tables](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2380776.2380786) (Kulkarni & Michels, SIGMOD Record 41(3), 2012) standardize it — notably, *constraints on system-versioned tables are enforced on current rows only*, the standard's own answer to your question: integrity obligations bind the current slice, history is evidence. The s37 defect is, in this vocabulary, a **mixed-timeline query**: membership computed non-sequenced (all-time), answerability computed current. The field's verdict is unambiguous — that mixture is a bug class, and the fix is committing the debt view to the current timeline. *(Confidence: high; the SQL:2011 constraint point verified this session.)*

**Bears directly — deontic logic and norm compliance.** A violations register with typed answering acts is a textbook contrary-to-duty structure: a violated primary norm triggers a reparational obligation, and a violation whose reparation is fulfilled is a *legally acceptable state*, distinct from both compliance and non-compliance. [Governatori & Rotolo's "Logic of Violations"](https://ojs.victoria.ac.nz/ajl/article/view/1780) (Australasian Journal of Logic, 2006) formalizes this with reparation chains (their ⊗ connective), and the derived [business-process-compliance line of work](http://www.governatori.net/papers/2013/handbookBPM.pdf) distinguishes compliant / compliant-with-a-compensated-violation / non-compliant. Two imports for your case: first, "debt" = an *uncompensated* violation — so your Stop hook is checking compensation status, a current-truth property, corroborating part 2B from an independent field; second, the witnessed defect is, in their terms, a norm whose reparation chain unintentionally terminates in an unfulfillable obligation — a mis-specified norm, not a mis-behaving subject. Removing the defective state of affairs (your retraction) is a recognized form of compensation. *(Confidence: high that the works exist and say this; the mapping onto s37 is my synthesis. Background: Chisholm's 1963 contrary-to-duty paradox and Carmo & Jones's handbook treatment, cited from knowledge, not re-fetched — medium.)*

**Bears, supporting — event sourcing.** [Fowler's Event Sourcing and Retroactive Event](https://martinfowler.com/eaaDev/RetroactiveEvent.html) patterns: corrections enter as new events; every projection is a pure function of the corrected stream. A projection computed partly from the raw stream and partly from the folded one is exactly the anti-pattern; the debt view must be a single fold. *(Confidence: medium-high.)* Double-entry bookkeeping practice is the same shape older: an erroneous entry is corrected by a reversing entry, both stay in the books, and no residual liability survives the reversal — retraction-as-answer is centuries-old domain practice. *(Cited as practice, no single quotable clause — medium.)*

**Consulted / considered and judged NOT to bear.** (a) **Abstract argumentation** (Dung 1995) and its *reinstatement* semantics — where attacking an attacker revives the attacked — is the intuition nearest to "supersede the superseder," and it is exactly what s31 deliberately ratified *against* (reinstatement-free). Named so nobody imports it by instinct; it clarifies the boundary, it does not govern. *(From knowledge — high confidence in the source, deliberate non-application.)* (b) **AGM belief revision**: governs rational *contraction* of belief sets; an append-only ledger never contracts, and the question here is obligation bookkeeping, not belief. (c) **Tamper-evident/audit-log integrity literature** (hash chains, WORM): mechanism-level, already covered by s26/s27, silent on debt semantics. (d) **Right-to-erasure / redaction in append-only stores**: destructive correction, excluded by BRIEF I3 from the start. (e) The BRIEF's own regimes (Part 11, CAT, DO-178C problem reports) all demand correction-as-new-linked-event and a full disposition trail — this amendment satisfies them via `work_violation_history` — but none speaks to the specific lapse-vs-defeat question; they constrain, they do not decide.
