# Sweep: Formal methods in life-critical civil/transport infrastructure (storm-surge barriers, railway signalling/interlockings, metro automation)

## Standards cited

### IEC 61508 (functional safety of E/E/PE safety-related systems), SIL 4
- **Clause/locus:** Parts 1-3; safety lifecycle, safety requirements specification, V&V (IEC 61508-3 clause 7); Annex A/B technique tables per SIL
- **Requires recorded:** The Maeslantkering BOS is classified to the highest level, SIL 4 (per the WODES'22 paper). At SIL 4 the standard requires a documented safety lifecycle: the safety requirements specification, the allocation of safety functions and their target failure measures (here the quantified reliability targets: probability of unjustified fail-to-close and fail-to-open both < 10^-5), the techniques/measures actually applied with their SIL-recommendation rating (HR/R/NR), and V&V records demonstrating each requirement was verified. Formal methods are 'Highly Recommended' at SIL 4, so the record must show which formal method was used for which safety function and the evidence it produced.
- **Confidence:** high

### CENELEC EN 50126 / EN 50128 / EN 50129 (railway RAMS, software, and safety-related electronic systems)
- **Clause/locus:** EN 50128 SIL 0-4, normative technique tables (Annex A); EN 50129 Safety Case structure; tool classification T1/T2/T3
- **Requires recorded:** For railway signalling/interlockings and CBTC metro automation (Line 14, SACEM/RER A lineage) the record must include: the Software Requirements Specification with bidirectional requirements->design->code->test traceability; Software Verification and Validation reports; the per-SIL technique table (formal methods and formal proof are Highly Recommended at SIL 3/4) with justification for each technique's selection; a Software Quality Assurance record; independence of the Verifier/Validator/Assessor roles; and tool classification with qualification evidence for T2 (verification) and T3 (code-generating, e.g. Atelier B translator to Ada) tools. All of this rolls up into the EN 50129 Safety Case, the final structured evidence document accepted before revenue service.
- **Confidence:** high

### B-method / Event-B (Abrial) with Atelier B / Rodin (RATP Paris Metro Line 14 'Meteor', Alstom/Siemens/ClearSy CBTC)
- **Clause/locus:** Abstract machine -> refinement -> implementation; proof obligations (POs); Rodin proving perspective status (discharged/reviewed/undischarged)
- **Requires recorded:** Record the formal models at each refinement level and the gluing invariants that relate abstract to concrete; the complete set of generated proof obligations and, for EACH, its discharge status and how it was discharged: automatically by the prover vs. discharged by interactive/manual proof vs. merely 'reviewed' (seen and postponed by a human, NOT proved) vs. undischarged. Rodin surfaces this as green (all discharged) / blue (all undischarged reviewed) / red (undischarged) - a 'reviewed' leaf is explicitly NOT yet proved and must be flagged as an admitted obligation. Line 14 'Meteor' is the canonical audit record: ~110,000 lines of B translated to ~86,000 lines of Ada, 27,800 proof obligations/lemmas of which ~90% discharged automatically by Atelier B and the remainder by interactive proof, with zero bugs found post-proof in functional/integration/on-site test or 25+ years of operation.
- **Confidence:** high

### Supervisory control theory (Ramadge-Wonham) synthesis with CIF / Eclipse ESCET and Supremica (Maeslantkering BOS redesign; waterway locks, movable bridges, road tunnels)
- **Clause/locus:** Plant model (EFAs) vs. requirements model; controllable/uncontrollable event partition; synthesis guarantees; CIF monolithic BDD synthesis
- **Requires recorded:** Record the plant model as a set of (extended) finite automata capturing UNcontrolled behavior; the requirements model as EFAs or event conditions ('sigma needs c' / 'c disables sigma') capturing desired behavior; the partition of the event set into controllable (Sigma_c, may be disabled by the supervisor, e.g. an actuator) and uncontrollable (Sigma_u, e.g. a sensor firing) events; and the synthesized supervisor together with the four properties it guarantees by construction - safety (all closed-loop behavior satisfies requirements), controllability (no uncontrollable event ever disabled), nonblockingness (a marked state reachable from every reachable state), and maximal permissiveness. For the BOS: 19 component models, 95 requirement models, controlled state space ~8e6 states / 4e7 transitions synthesized in seconds with the CIF toolset. Crucially, the record must capture the MODELING DECISIONS and their rationale (the 'Rosetta-stone' document giving each model in plain text + mathematical notation + CIF), including cases where the underlying requirement had to be recovered from a documented solution-path (e.g. the water-level/harbor-confirmation requirement that was corrected from 'needs confirmed' to 'needs confirmed OR sent' after expert discussion).
- **Confidence:** high

### Z notation (Spivey) + Promela/Spin model checking (original 1990s Maeslantkering BOS design)
- **Clause/locus:** Z schemas (state, invariants, pre/post-conditions); ZTC type-checker; Spin verification of critical subsystems
- **Requires recorded:** The historical BOS record shows the auditable artifacts of a specify-then-verify (option 1) approach: Z schemas formalizing the functional view (data structures, operations, invariants), type/syntax/completeness checking of all schemas with the ZTC type checker, manual evaluation of pre- and post-conditions for all subsystems, manual completeness checks for core/critical subsystems, and manual invariant checks for the highest-criticality subsystems; plus Promela behavioral models verified with the Spin model checker for the three critical subsystems (monitoring/controlling other subsystems, obtaining measurement data, controlling the surge-barrier PLC), with deadlock-freedom argued via the Martin-Welch rules. This documents WHICH verification steps were automated vs. done by manual inspection - itself an audit fact. A key lesson recorded: the original spec captured the intended solution path, not the underlying requirements, and the loss of Z/Promela expertise 30 years on now impedes maintenance - motivating the 'record the rationale, not just the artifact' discipline.
- **Confidence:** high

### RTCA DO-333 (Formal Methods Supplement to DO-178C/DO-278A) + DO-330 (Software Tool Qualification)
- **Clause/locus:** DO-333 formal-analysis objectives and soundness/assumption evidence; DO-330 tool qualification package
- **Requires recorded:** Cited as the mature cross-domain precedent for WHAT formal-methods assurance must record (originating in aviation but the recording obligations transfer to civil/transport). DO-333 requires: evidence of the SOUNDNESS of each formal method used (that it will never give unwarranted confidence / never claim a false property), and that ALL assumptions of each formal analysis be explicitly described and justified. Where a tool produces the verification evidence (a prover, model checker, or synthesizer), DO-330 requires a tool qualification package establishing the tool is dependable for its intended use - including the tool's operational requirements, the assumptions on its use, and verification of the tool itself. This is the standard's basis for demanding, for any proof, both its assumptions and the qualified provenance of the tool that produced it.
- **Confidence:** high

### GSN / Goal Structuring Notation and OMG SACM (structured assurance/safety cases)
- **Clause/locus:** Argument elements: Goal, Strategy, Solution (evidence), Context, Assumption, Justification
- **Requires recorded:** The safety/assurance case must be recorded as an explicit argument structure, not prose: top-level safety Goals decomposed via Strategies down to Solutions (the concrete evidence - proofs, model-checking results, test reports), with every Assumption and Justification made a first-class, labelled node and every Context (the environment/scope in which a goal holds) stated. This is the connective tissue that ties the formal-methods evidence above to the claims they support, and makes the assumptions on which each proof rests auditable rather than buried.
- **Confidence:** medium

### ALCOA+ data-integrity principles
- **Clause/locus:** Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, + Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available
- **Requires recorded:** Governs the INTEGRITY of the records themselves rather than their content. Every logged artifact must be Attributable (who/which agent produced it, and when), Legible, Contemporaneous (recorded at the time of the act, not reconstructed after), Original (or a verified true copy), Accurate, and additionally Complete (no cherry-picking - the whole run, including failed proof attempts and discharged counterexamples), Consistent, Enduring, and Available for audit. Originates in pharma/GxP (FDA/MHRA) so the label is cross-domain, but the principles are the recognized bar for record integrity and map directly onto an auditable AI-collaborator log.
- **Confidence:** medium

## Loggable items

- Record the formal specification artifact itself (the Z schemas / Event-B machines / EFA plant+requirements models / interlocking model) at every version, and pin the exact version/hash used for each proof run.
- Record the environment/assumption model: every assumption the proof rests on (sensor behavior, event uncontrollability, timing discretization, forecast inputs) AND its validity bounds - the conditions under which the assumption ceases to hold.
- Record requirements->specification traceability bidirectionally: which formal spec element discharges which safety requirement, and flag any requirement not covered by a formal element.
- Record, for EACH proof obligation, its discharge status and MEANS: proved automatically, proved by interactive/manual proof, merely 'reviewed'/postponed (i.e. admitted, not proved), or undischarged - never let 'reviewed' masquerade as proved.
- Record aggregate proof metrics per model: total POs generated, count automatic vs. interactive vs. reviewed vs. open, so an auditor sees the automation ratio (e.g. Meteor's ~90% automatic of 27,800).
- Record every refinement step and its gluing invariant relating abstract to concrete model, and that the refinement POs discharged.
- Record, for a synthesized supervisor, the controllable/uncontrollable event partition and the four guaranteed properties (safety, controllability, nonblockingness, maximal permissiveness), plus the resulting controlled state-space size.
- Record every counterexample found by a model checker or during synthesis, and its resolution (spec change / requirement correction / assumption added) - not just the final green result.
- Record the rationale behind each modeling decision, including underlying requirements recovered from a documented solution-path (why an event ordering is required, not merely that it is), so the spec is maintainable after expert knowledge decays.
- Record tool identity, version, configuration, and qualification status for every tool in the evidence chain (prover, model checker, synthesizer, type checker, code generator), and which class (T1/T2/T3) it is under the applicable railway/DO-330 scheme.
- Record which verification steps were automated vs. performed by manual inspection (e.g. Z pre/post-condition and invariant checks done by hand), since the assurance strength differs.
- Record the SIL/assurance-level classification of each subsystem and the quantified target failure measures it must meet (e.g. fail-to-close/fail-to-open probability < 10^-5).
- Record the soundness basis of each formal method used (that it cannot return a false 'verified'), per DO-333.
- Record the assurance-case argument structure (GSN/SACM): goals, strategies, evidence solutions, contexts, and explicitly labelled assumptions and justifications linking evidence to safety claims.
- Record record-integrity metadata for every artifact (ALCOA+): author/agent identity, contemporaneous timestamp, originality/true-copy status, and ensure the capture is complete (whole run, including failed attempts) and enduringly available for audit.

## Integrity principles

- A proof is only as strong as its assumptions: log the assumption set and its validity bounds alongside every discharged obligation, never the QED alone.
- 'Reviewed' or 'admitted' is not 'proved': the record must distinguish machine-checked discharge from human-postponed obligations, and count them separately.
- Completeness over curation (ALCOA+ 'Complete'): capture the whole run including failed proof attempts, counterexamples, and the requirement corrections they forced - deciding what is 'worth keeping' is how audit trails get holes.
- Traceability is bidirectional: every safety requirement must map to a formal element that discharges it, and every formal element back to a requirement; orphans on either side are defects to flag.
- Record the rationale, not just the artifact: the underlying 'why' of a spec (recovered requirement behind a solution-path) is what keeps it auditable and maintainable after the originating expertise decays - the explicit lesson of the 30-year-old BOS Z specs.
- The evidence chain is only as trustworthy as its tools: qualify and version-pin every prover, checker, synthesizer, and code generator, and record the qualification basis (DO-330 / EN 50128 tool class).
- State soundness explicitly: record why the chosen formal method cannot return a false 'verified', so an auditor need not assume it.
- Records must be attributable and contemporaneous: who/which agent produced each artifact and at the time of the act, not reconstructed afterward.

## Formal-methods notes

This domain's assurance is specification-and-proof, not test coverage, so the record's spine is the formal artifact plus the discharge state of every obligation it generates. Four things must be inseparable in the log: (1) the formal specification/model at a pinned version; (2) the environment/assumption model with explicit validity bounds - a proof is only as good as its assumptions, so each assumption and the conditions under which it fails must be recorded (DO-333 requires all assumptions be described and justified); (3) per-obligation discharge evidence distinguishing automatic proof, interactive/manual proof, 'reviewed'/admitted (seen but NOT proved), and undischarged - conflating 'reviewed' with 'proved' is the characteristic audit hole; and (4) tool provenance and qualification for whatever produced the evidence (DO-330 tool package; EN 50128 T2/T3 tool classification). Synthesis-based formal methods (Ramadge-Wonham supervisory control via CIF/ESCET/Supremica) shift the recording emphasis: correctness is guaranteed by construction from the plant model + requirements model + controllable/uncontrollable partition, so the auditable objects become those input models, the recovered underlying requirements (vs. documented solution-paths), the four guaranteed properties, and every counterexample surfaced and resolved during modeling. The Maeslantkering BOS (SIL 4 per IEC 61508; originally Z + Promela/Spin, now supervisory-control-synthesis) and the RATP Line 14 'Meteor' B-method development (27,800 POs, ~90% automatic, 110k lines B -> 86k lines Ada, EN 50128/50129 Safety Case) are the two canonical, real records for this spine - civil/transport, and first-class rather than footnotes to aerospace. Overarching this, GSN/SACM structures the argument and ALCOA+ governs the integrity of the records themselves.
