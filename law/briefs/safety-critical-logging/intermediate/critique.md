# Adversarial Critique of the Synthesis Draft

## Unsupported or dubious citations

- arXiv:2504.08518 is over-attributed and miscited. Fetching the paper shows it is an mCRL2 re-verification study (~5,000-line spec, 40 behavioral properties, state space 4.98x10^14). It does NOT contain: (a) a SIL 4 classification, (b) any <10^-5 fail-to-close/fail-to-open figure, (c) PVS, or (d) 'challenge theorems'. Yet the draft cites this single paper as verification for all four. SIL 4 + Z/Promela/Spin origin actually trace to the original Wijbrans/Kars BOS work and WODES'22; PVS + spec/code divergence trace to Madlener/Smetsers/van Eekelen, 'A Formal Verification Study on the Rotterdam Storm Surge Barrier' (Springer 2010). The draft's opening 'Both spine cases verified' and the §1.1/§5 'Confidence: high - verified against arXiv:2504.08518' overstate what that source supports.
- 'probability of unjustified fail-to-close and fail-to-open both < 10^-5' (§1.1, propagated into G3 'target failure measure e.g. <10^-5'): could not corroborate. Public sourcing on the barrier gives a ~10^-4 / once-per-10,000-year reliability target, not 10^-5, and the cited arXiv paper states no such number. This is presented as high-confidence but is the least-grounded quantitative claim in the document; either re-source to the confidential Rijkswaterstaat requirement (declared confidential per 2504.08518, so likely un-citable) or downgrade to unverified.
- Model counts '19 component / 95 requirement models, ~8x10^6 states / 4x10^7 transitions' (§1.1 F8): hedged as 'as reported in WODES'22', which is fair, but the draft blends two distinct recent studies into one 'BESW' storyline - the CIF/ESCET/Supremica supervisory-control synthesis redesign (WODES'22 figures) and the separate mCRL2 re-verification (2504.08518, whose state space is 4.98x10^14, six-plus orders of magnitude larger). Presenting them as a single verified spine invites a reader to cross-attribute figures. Separate the two provenances explicitly.
- Ramadge-Wonham 'four properties it guarantees by construction: safety, controllability, nonblockingness, and maximal permissiveness' (§1.1 F8): canonical RW synthesis guarantees a trio - controllability, nonblockingness, maximal permissiveness. 'Safety' as a fourth by-construction property is the CIF/ESCET requirement-satisfaction framing, not RW proper. Defensible under ESCET but should be labeled as the ESCET/Supremica characterization, not attributed to Ramadge-Wonham theory.
- Meteor Line 14 figures (110k B -> 86k Ada, 27,800 lemmas, ~90% auto, zero post-proof bugs since Oct 1998): independently corroborated via CLEARSY and arXiv:2005.07190. No issue - this spine case is solid.
- SEC 17a-4 2023 audit-trail alternative + D3P, WHO TRS 1033, DO-333/DO-330 TQL, EN 50128 T1-T3, ISO 26262 TCL: spot-checked and real; the draft's paraphrase-not-quote posture is appropriate. BS 5975 'Cat 3' is really a temporary-works design-check category conflated with IStructE checking categories - minor, and already flagged medium.

## Coverage gaps

- Timing / WCET and resource-bound records are absent. For hard-real-time interlockings and barrier/tunnel control, worst-case execution time and stack/heap/memory bounds are life-critical record obligations (EN 50128, DO-178C schedulability, IEC 60880). Nothing in G1-G12 or F1-F13 obliges recording them - a proof of functional correctness that says nothing about meeting deadlines is exactly the kind of unstated-assumption hazard I7 is meant to catch.
- Common-cause / common-mode failure analysis and redundant-channel independence are not captured. The Maeslantkering BOS and railway interlockings rely on redundancy/diversity; ARP4761 CMA and IEC 60880 CCF records are the evidence that redundancy is real. ARP4761 is mentioned only as an SSA interface; no register entry records CCF/CMA or the diversity argument. This is a first-class civil/nuclear/aero requirement dropped in synthesis.
- Cybersecurity records for safety-critical I&C are entirely absent (IEC 62443, EN 50129 security-informed safety, DO-326A/ED-202A). In-scope domains - networked exchanges, barrier control - have a security-safety coupling; a modern life-critical audit record cannot omit security-relevant change/assessment entries without at least an explicit out-of-scope declaration.
- Human-factors / operator-in-the-loop / HMI safety records are dropped. A documented Maeslant lesson (the human-factors literature on this exact barrier) is that automation choices were driven by operator-reliability concerns. No G/F entry records HMI hazards or operator-procedure verification.
- Personnel competence/qualification records get only a parenthetical ('competence noted' in I2, 'assessor competence' in §1.4) but no dedicated register entry, though IEC 61508 §6 and ISO 26262 make competence-of-persons a required work product.
- Proof-artifact (proof-script / proof-object) retention is under-specified. F4/F5 record discharge status and counts; F13 records a retention *plan*; but no entry obliges storing the actual proof scripts/objects so an inspector can re-run the prover decades later. 'Re-verifiable' (I11/F13) is asserted without an entry that captures the re-verification inputs.

## Non-operationalizable items

- G10 (Flagged out-of-scope hazard): trigger is 'the collaborator notices a hazard'. Noticing is not an observable event, so the draft's own defect rule ('trigger fired but entry absent = defect') cannot fire - a hazard that was present but never noticed produces no detectable missing entry. This is aspirational (the mother's-life bar) rather than a mechanizable trigger+field; it should be labeled as such, not listed alongside machine-detectable entries like G1.
- F10 (Soundness basis): the field 'the documented argument that the method cannot return a false verified' has no defined form, author, or acceptance criterion. What makes a soundness argument sufficient is exactly the hard part and is left unspecified, so the entry cannot be checked for presence-vs-adequacy - it can be satisfied by a sentence. Needs a concrete required structure (e.g. tool's soundness pedigree + the specific abstraction gap + why the gap is conservative) to be implementable.
- F11 (Challenge / validation theorem): trigger 'the spec itself is validated against engineer intent' has no firing point - there is no event that obliges it, so absence is never a defect. The field 'phrased so a non-specialist can map them to the natural-language goal' is a quality aspiration, not a loggable value. As written it is optional-by-construction.
- The register's central claimed guarantee - 'contemporaneity is enforced by making the trigger-to-entry gap detectable' (§4) - only holds for harness-observable triggers (G1 tool calls, F4 PO-status changes, F6 tool runs). For judgment-triggered entries (G3 rationale, G10, F11, F2 assumption-noticing) there is no independent oracle that the trigger fired, so gap-detection silently fails there. The draft should scope the 'detectable gap' property to machine-observable triggers and stop implying it covers the judgment-based ones.

## Formal-methods weight check (aerospace-default question)

Not underweighted - the opposite. The draft deliberately foregrounds civil/transport formal methods as 'the spine' (§0, §1.1) and explicitly demotes aerospace (DO-178C/333/330) to 'cross-domain precedent, not footnotes to aerospace'. Structurally the formal-methods block F1-F13 is the richest part of the register, and I7 (assumptions with validity bounds) + I9 (discharge-status honesty) are correctly identified as the FM-specific invariants a generic/aerospace-testing regime would miss. The only leak of testing/aero framing is that the verification vocabulary in G6 and §1.2 (DAL, MC/DC, Tables A-5/6/7) is aerospace-derived, but that is used as legitimate cross-domain precedent, not as a default. If anything the rhetorical over-correction ('not footnotes to aerospace', the §2 cluster-count 'robustness' argument the draft itself concedes is rhetorical not statistical) is a bigger stylistic risk than any aerospace default.

## Verdict

Solid, well-disciplined draft with one genuine citation-integrity defect that must be fixed before circulation: arXiv:2504.08518 (an mCRL2 study) is cited as high-confidence verification for four claims it does not contain (SIL 4, <10^-5 fail probabilities, PVS, challenge theorems), and the <10^-5 figure appears to be simply wrong (public target is ~10^-4). Under the project's own 'never assert unsubstantiated empirical claims' bar and the draft's own §5 discipline, that miscitation is the priority fix - re-attribute PVS/challenge-theorems to Madlener et al. 2010 and SIL4/Z/Promela to WODES'22/Wijbrans, and downgrade the 10^-5 claim to unverified. Meteor Line 14 is fully corroborated. Secondary: three register entries (G10, F10, F11) are aspirational rather than mechanizable and the '§4 detectable-gap' guarantee should be scoped to machine-observable triggers; and six life-critical record classes (WCET/resource bounds, common-cause failure, cybersecurity, human factors, competence, proof-artifact retention) are missing or under-specified. FM/civil weighting is not the problem - it is correctly first-class.
