# Sweep: Data-integrity & scientific/forensic record model: ALCOA(+), controlled lab-notebook practice, chain of custody, and reproducibility records for life-critical, formal-methods-grade AI-collaborator development

## Standards cited

### FDA "Data Integrity and Compliance with Drug CGMP: Questions and Answers" (Guidance for Industry, Dec 2018) — https://www.fda.gov/media/119267/download
- **Clause/locus:** Q&A on definitions of data integrity, ALCOA, and the CGMP data life cycle (21 CFR Parts 210/211/212)
- **Requires recorded:** Every record touching the CGMP life cycle (creation, modification, processing, maintenance, archival, retrieval, transmission, disposition) must be Attributable (who/what system did it), Legible, Contemporaneous (logged AT the time of the act, not reconstructed later), Original (or a certified true copy — no transcription-then-discard of the source), and Accurate (matches what actually happened, including any error). For an AI-collaborator this maps to: log the actual prompt/tool-call/diff at the instant it is issued and by which actor (human vs which model/tool invocation), not a paraphrase assembled afterward.
- **Confidence:** high

### MHRA "'GXP' Data Integrity Guidance and Definitions", Revision 1 (March 2018)
- **Clause/locus:** Sections on ALCOA+ (the 'plus' elements) and risk-based data lifecycle governance
- **Requires recorded:** Extends ALCOA with Complete (nothing selectively dropped, including repeated/failed attempts and out-of-spec results), Consistent (chronology and sequencing of entries must be self-consistent, e.g. timestamps that cannot be reordered after the fact), Enduring (recorded on a durable medium for the full retention period, not a volatile/pruneable log), and Available (retrievable/readable for the entire retention period, including by an inspector who was not present at creation). Directly informs the CLAUDE.md 'persist the ephemera' clause: workflow/subagent transcripts under ~/.claude or /tmp are exactly the Enduring/Available failure mode MHRA is targeting.
- **Confidence:** high

### WHO Technical Report Series No. 996 (2016), Annex 5, "Guidance on Good Data and Record Management Practices" — superseded 2021 by TRS 1033 "Guideline on Data Integrity"
- **Clause/locus:** Definitions section (ALCOA) and data-governance/lifecycle sections
- **Requires recorded:** Data governance arrangements proportionate to risk and criticality of the record; formal definitions of raw/source data and metadata that must travel with any derived record. Note: cite TRS 996 for the ALCOA definitions used historically, but flag that the current authoritative WHO text is TRS 1033 (2021) — use that as the live reference, not 996, if precision on 'current WHO position' is required.
- **Confidence:** medium

### 21 CFR Part 11 — Electronic Records; Electronic Signatures
- **Clause/locus:** §11.10(e) audit trail requirement
- **Requires recorded:** Secure, computer-generated, time-stamped audit trails that independently record the date/time of operator entries and actions creating, modifying, or deleting an electronic record; audit trail must capture who, what action, when, and (per FDA guidance interpretation) why; record changes must not obscure previously recorded information (no destructive edit-in-place); no user — including admins — may modify the audit trail itself; retained at least as long as the record it covers. Maps directly to: an AI collaborator's own transcript/log must be append-only and tamper-evident, and a later 'clarification' or correction must be a new entry referencing the old one, never an edit that erases it.
- **Confidence:** high

### ALCOA+ (industry-standard mnemonic, not itself a single regulation — codified across FDA/MHRA/WHO/EMA guidance above)
- **Clause/locus:** N/A (cross-cutting principle set)
- **Requires recorded:** Nine properties as a checklist for every logged item: Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available. Use this as the per-record acceptance test in the loggable_items list below rather than as a standalone citation.
- **Confidence:** high

## Loggable items

- Record every prompt, tool call, and tool result at the moment it is issued/returned — not a post-hoc summary written after the session — with a wall-clock timestamp and the identity of the issuing actor (human user, specific model/version, specific subagent) — this is the Attributable + Contemporaneous pair; a paraphrased-after-the-fact transcript fails Contemporaneous even if factually correct.
- Persist ALL subagent/background-task transcripts, workflow-script output, and run journals into versioned, checksummed storage (per this repo's own tools/persist_claude_ephemera.py convention) rather than leaving them in ~/.claude or /tmp — this is the Enduring + Available pair; a log that is technically produced but lives only in a prunable/volatile location has not actually been recorded for audit purposes.
- Record negative results and failed attempts with the same rigor as successes: a rejected design, a proof attempt that did not close, a test that failed and was later fixed by a DIFFERENT change than first guessed — omitting these is the single most common Completeness violation and is exactly the failure mode ALCOA+'s 'Complete' element targets.
- When a record is corrected, append a new dated entry that supersedes the old one and states why; never edit or delete the original entry in place — this is the Original + audit-trail-integrity requirement (21 CFR 11.10(e)): 'record changes shall not obscure previously recorded information.'
- Log the exact chain of custody for any artifact that feeds a life-critical decision: which file/data at which hash/version was read, by which process, and what was derived from it — so provenance can be walked back to source without gaps, the forensic/chain-of-custody analogue of ALCOA's Original.
- Log disagreements or overrides explicitly: if a human's stated observation is used in place of (or to correct) an automated/inferred state, record both the inference and the override with attribution — never silently downgrade an observed fact to fit a stale inferred one (this mirrors both Attributable and Accurate).
- For any formal-methods artifact (spec, proof obligation, proof script, model-checker run), log it as its own contemporaneous record, not folded into a narrative summary — see formal_methods_notes for the specific items.
- Maintain internal consistency of timestamps/sequence across logs from different subsystems (workflow script vs subagent vs git commit) so the full session can be reconstructed in true order — the Consistent element; a log whose ordering can't be trusted is as bad as one that's incomplete.
- Record who/what has READ or exported a given life-critical record (access log), not just who wrote it — Available is not satisfied by 'exists somewhere', it requires demonstrable retrievability, which in an audit context includes proof that retrieval actually works, not just that a file exists.
- Where the AI collaborator itself judged something to be a hazard-but-out-of-scope-so-flagged (per this project's 'mother's-life bar' engineering-responsibility clause), log that flag as its own contemporaneous record independent of whether the flagged item was later acted on — a flag that only lives in conversational memory and not in a durable record has not actually been raised for audit purposes.

## Formal-methods notes

This domain's ALCOA+/chain-of-custody lens applies to formal-methods artifacts as records, not as narrative: (1) the FORMAL SPECIFICATION itself (e.g. a B-method/Event-B model, an Alloy/TLA+ spec, a type signature under this repo's ADR-0000 type-driven-design law) must be versioned and timestamped as an Original record at the moment it is authored/changed — a spec reconstructed from memory after the proof was done fails Contemporaneous; (2) every PROOF OBLIGATION generated by the toolchain (e.g. Rodin/Atelier-B PO list, a Z3/SMT unsat-core, a Coq/Isabelle theorem statement) must be logged as a discrete record with its own identity, not summarized as 'all proofs passed' — Complete means the FAILED or UNDISCHARGED obligations are logged with equal prominence, exactly analogous to negative-result logging in a lab notebook, and exactly what this repo's own '(GLiNER 23-vs-7 confound)' and 'Z3 unsat-core' ephemera entries already model; (3) VERIFICATION EVIDENCE — the actual prover/model-checker transcript (tool name, version, invocation args, wall-clock run, exit status) — is itself a chain-of-custody item: which spec-hash was fed to which tool-version to produce which proof artifact, so the proof can't be silently re-run against a changed spec without the record showing it; (4) TOOL QUALIFICATION records (per the DO-178C/DO-330-style discipline, cross-referenced by the correctness/soundness sibling domain) belong here as Attributable+Original records of exactly which verified-tool version produced the evidence, since ALCOA's 'Attributable' extends to tools, not only humans; (5) assumptions and scope limits stated in the spec (what the model does NOT cover) must be logged as explicitly as the spec's positive claims — an unstated assumption is an Incompleteness violation with the same audit weight as a missing negative test result."
