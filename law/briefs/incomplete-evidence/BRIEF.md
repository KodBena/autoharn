# Reference Brief: Standards Naming "Presenting an Incomplete Record as an Evidence Deliverable"

**The conduct at issue (neutral statement):** A collaborator asked to produce the evidence base for an independent audit assembled and handed over an "audit evidence" package that included the partial, still-being-written output of a process that had not finished running. The incomplete record was labeled "point-in-time snapshot / still running" but was bundled into the evidence set and presented as the deliverable, rather than (a) delivering only the completed portion clearly scoped as such, (b) waiting for completion, or (c) withholding until whole. Core alleged violation: presenting an incomplete artifact as a usable audit/evidence deliverable, corrupting the evidence base because a consumer cannot tell what is missing.

> Verbatim quotes are marked with quotation marks and were retrieved from the cited source. Where exact wording could not be retrieved, this is stated explicitly and the substance is paraphrased. No standard numbers or quotations were invented.

## 1. Auditing

### PCAOB AS 1105 — Audit Evidence (completeness assertion)
- **Body / number / title:** PCAOB, AS 1105, *Audit Evidence*
- **What it requires:** the "completeness" assertion demands that everything that should appear in the record actually appears.
- **Quote (¶.11, verbatim):** "*Completeness*—All transactions and accounts that should be presented in the financial statements are so included."
- **URL:** https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS1105

### PCAOB AS 1105 — sufficiency & appropriateness of evidence
- **What it requires:** evidence must be sufficient (quantity) and appropriate (quality = relevance + reliability) to provide a reasonable basis for a conclusion; an incomplete, still-changing artifact is neither.
- **Quotes (verbatim):**
  - ¶.04 "The auditor must plan and perform audit procedures to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to provide a reasonable basis for his or her opinion."
  - ¶.05 "Sufficiency is the measure of the quantity of audit evidence."
  - ¶.06 "Appropriateness is the measure of the quality of audit evidence, i.e., its relevance and reliability."
- **URL:** https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS1105

### IAASB ISA 500 — Audit Evidence (international counterpart)
- **Body / number / title:** IAASB, ISA 500, *Audit Evidence*
- **What it requires:** obtain "sufficient appropriate audit evidence" — sufficiency = quantity, appropriateness = quality (relevance + reliability).
- **Quotes (verbatim):**
  - ¶2 "The auditor should obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to be able to draw reasonable conclusions on which to base the audit opinion."
  - ¶7 "Sufficiency is the measure of the quantity of audit evidence. Appropriateness is the measure of the quality of audit evidence; that is, its relevance and its reliability in providing support for, or detecting misstatements in, the classes of transactions, account balances, and disclosures and related assertions."
- **URL:** primary PDF https://www.icjce.es/images/pdfs/tecnica2/normativainternacional/isa500.pdf ; IAASB landing https://www.iaasb.org/consultations-projects/isa-500-series

### ISA 315 (Revised) — completeness assertion definition
- **What it requires:** completeness — all transactions and events that should have been recorded have been recorded, and all related disclosures that should have been included have been included (paraphrase per IAASB/AICPA assertion set; verbatim standard text not retrieved).
- **URL:** https://www.accaglobal.com/gb/en/student/exam-support-resources/fundamentals-exams-study-resources/f8/technical-articles/assertions.html

### PCAOB AS 1215 — Audit Documentation ("experienced auditor" test)
- **Body / number / title:** PCAOB, AS 1215, *Audit Documentation*
- **What it requires:** the working-paper record must be self-sufficient to an independent reviewer — the exact failure mode when a partial, in-progress artifact is handed over.
- **Quote (¶.06A, verbatim):** "Audit documentation must contain sufficient information to enable an experienced auditor, having no previous connection with the engagement: (1) To understand the nature, timing, extent, and results of the procedures performed, evidence obtained, and conclusions reached, and (2) To determine who performed the work and the date such work was completed…"
- **URL:** https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS1215

### IAASB ISA 230 — Audit Documentation (international counterpart)
- **What it requires:** documentation must give an "experienced auditor, having no previous connection with the audit" enough to understand the work; prepared "on a timely basis."
- **Quotes (verbatim):**
  - ¶9 "The auditor should prepare the audit documentation so as to enable an experienced auditor, having no previous connection with the audit, to understand: (a) The nature, timing, and extent of the audit procedures performed…"
  - ¶2 "The auditor should prepare, on a timely basis, audit documentation that provides: (a) A sufficient and appropriate record of the basis for the auditor's report; and (b) Evidence that the audit was performed in accordance with ISAs and applicable legal and regulatory requirements."
- **URL:** https://www.icjce.es/images/pdfs/tecnica2/normativainternacional/isa230.pdf

### GAO Yellow Book / GAGAS — evidence standard
- **Body / number / title:** U.S. GAO, *Government Auditing Standards* (GAGAS / "Yellow Book"), 2018 and 2024 revisions
- **What it requires:** findings and conclusions must rest on "sufficient, appropriate evidence"; and evidence is "sufficient and appropriate" only when it is, among other things, **complete** with respect to the audit objectives.
- **Quotes (verbatim, 2018 Revision, performance-audit standards):**
  - ¶8.90 "Auditors must obtain sufficient, appropriate evidence to provide a reasonable basis for addressing the audit objectives and supporting their findings and conclusions."
  - ¶8.12 sufficient, appropriate evidence "provides auditors with a reasonable basis for findings and conclusions that are valid, accurate, appropriate, and complete with respect to the audit objectives."
  - ¶8.13 "The sufficiency and appropriateness of evidence needed and tests of evidence are determined by the auditors based on the audit objectives, findings, and conclusions."
- **URL:** https://www.gao.gov/yellowbook ; 2018 accessible PDF https://www.gao.gov/assets/700/694881.pdf ; 2024 revision PDF https://guides.gaoinnovations.gov/yellowbook/2024/GAO-24-106786.pdf

## 2. Data Integrity (GxP / ALCOA+)

### MHRA "GXP" Data Integrity Guidance and Definitions (March 2018) — ALCOA+ "Complete"
- **Body / number / title:** UK Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA), *GXP Data Integrity Guidance and Definitions*, Rev 1, March 2018
- **What it requires:** data must be whole; a point-in-time partial capture is by definition not "Complete," and "Data" itself is defined to require full and complete reconstruction of the activity.
- **Quotes (verbatim, §6 / §3.1 / §6.1):**
  - "**Complete – the data must be whole; a complete set.**"
  - Definition of Data: "All original records and true copies of original records, including source data and metadata and all subsequent transformations and reports of these data… that… allow full and complete reconstruction and evaluation of the GXP activity."
  - §3.1: "The organisational culture should ensure data is complete, consistent and accurate in all its forms."
- **URL:** https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/5aa2b9ede5274a3e391e37f3/MHRA_GxP_data_integrity_guide_March_edited_Final.pdf

### MHRA — "Original / Raw data" (preliminary-vs-final distinction)
- **What it requires:** the "Original record / Raw data" is the first-capture; derived, partial, or in-progress reports are not the original evidence and cannot substitute for it.
- **Quote (verbatim, §6.2):** "Raw data is defined as the original record (data) which can be described as the first-capture of information… Raw data must permit full reconstruction of the activities."
- **URL:** same as above.

### ALCOA / ALCOA+ framework and FDA recognition
- **Body:** ALCOA (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate) + Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available — referenced by FDA (2018 *Data Integrity and Compliance With Drug CGMP* guidance), EMA, WHO, MHRA.
- **What it requires:** "Complete" = all data recorded, with no omissions.
- **Quote:** FDA "Complete" definition not retrieved verbatim from the FDA primary text; MHRA verbatim above is the cleanest primary source.
- **URL:** https://totallab.com/resources/alcoa-principles/ (secondary overview)

## 3. Records Management

### ISO 15489-1:2016 — Information and documentation — Records management
- **Body / number / title:** ISO 15489-1:2016
- **What it requires:** an authoritative record must have four characteristics — authenticity, reliability, integrity, usability — where **integrity** means the record is "complete and unaltered." A partial, still-being-written artifact fails the integrity characteristic.
- **Quotes (verbatim from the official ISO sample, clauses 5.2.2.1–5.2.2.2):**
  - §5.2.1(b) "records, regardless of form or structure, are authoritative evidence of business when they possess the characteristics of authenticity, reliability, integrity and useability."
  - §5.2.2.1 Authenticity: "An authentic record is one that can be proven to: a) be what it purports to be; b) have been created or sent by the agent purported to have created or sent it; and c) have been created or sent when purported."
  - §5.2.2.2 Reliability: "A reliable record is one: a) whose contents can be trusted as a full and accurate representation of the transactions, activities or facts to which they attest; and b) which can be depended upon in the course of subsequent transactions or activities."
- **Integrity (§5.2.2.3) / Usability (§5.2.2.4):** the standard's own clause text was beyond the 12-page public sample; ISO 15489 defines **integrity** as the record being "complete and unaltered" (widely-cited formulation, not retrieved verbatim here) and **usability** as a record that can be located, retrieved, presented and interpreted.
- **URL:** official landing https://www.iso.org/standard/62542.html ; official sample PDF https://cdn.standards.iteh.ai/samples/62542/fe383f4fe10448d5b22ce628b1542ed6/ISO-15489-1-2016.pdf ; DCC briefing https://www.dcc.ac.uk/guidance/briefing-papers/standards-watch-papers/iso-15489

## 4. Digital Evidence / Chain of Custody / Forensics

### ISO/IEC 27037:2012 — Identification, collection, acquisition and preservation of digital evidence
- **Body / number / title:** ISO/IEC 27037:2012
- **What it requires:** handling processes must preserve the "integrity, authenticity, and reliability" of potential digital evidence and be governed by the principles of **auditability, repeatability, reproducibility, and justifiability**; the chain of custody and all processes applied must be recorded so that spoliation cannot be alleged.
- **Quote:** exact clause text not retrieved (iso.org returned HTTP 403). Operative principles (auditability/repeatability/reproducibility/justifiability) confirmed via secondary sources.
- **URL:** https://www.iso.org/standard/44381.html

### ISO/IEC 27042:2015 — Analysis and interpretation of digital evidence
- Companion standard governing that analysis be complete and defensible.
- **URL:** https://www.iso.org/standard/44405.html

### ACPO Good Practice Guide for Digital Evidence (UK)
- **Body:** (former) Association of Chief Police Officers, *Good Practice Guide for Digital Evidence*, v5
- **What it requires:** the four ACPO principles — notably that an audit trail of all processes be created and preserved so that an independent third party can examine them and reach the same result. Directly targets the "consumer cannot tell what is missing" harm.
- **Quotes (verbatim):**
  - Principle 1: "No action taken by law enforcement agencies, persons employed within those agencies or their agents should change data which may subsequently be relied upon in court."
  - Principle 3: "An audit trail or other record of all processes applied to digital evidence should be created and preserved… an independent third party should be able to examine those processes and achieve the same result."
- **URL:** https://forensiccontrol.com/guides/acpo-guidelines-principles-explained/

## 5. Legal — Rule of Completeness

### Federal Rule of Evidence 106 — Remainder of or Related Statements
- **Body / number / title:** U.S. Federal Rules of Evidence, Rule 106 (as amended Dec. 1, 2023)
- **What it requires:** a party may not introduce a partial statement that creates a misleading impression; the adverse party may require the remainder that "in fairness ought to be considered at the same time." The animating concern is precisely "the misleading impression created by taking matters out of context."
- **Quote (verbatim, current text):** "If a party introduces all or part of a statement, an adverse party may require the introduction, at that time, of any other part — or any other statement — that in fairness ought to be considered at the same time. The adverse party may do so over a hearsay objection."
- **URL:** https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/fre/rule_106 ; doctrine https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/doctrine_of_completeness

## 6. Professional Responsibility / Ethics

### IESBA International Code of Ethics for Professional Accountants — Fundamental Principles
- **Body / number / title:** IFAC / International Ethics Standards Board for Accountants (IESBA), *International Code of Ethics for Professional Accountants*
- **What it requires:** **Integrity** — "straightforward and honest," not to be knowingly associated with information that is misleading or presented recklessly; **Professional Competence and Due Care** — act "diligently and in accordance with applicable technical and professional standards." Presenting incomplete, in-progress output as a finished deliverable offends both.
- **Quotes (verbatim, 2024 Handbook):**
  - R111.1 "A professional accountant shall comply with the principle of integrity, which requires an accountant to be straightforward and honest in all professional and business relationships."
  - R111.2 "A professional accountant shall not knowingly be associated with reports, returns, communications or other information where the accountant believes that the information: (a) Contains a materially false or misleading statement; (b) Contains statements or information provided recklessly; or (c) Omits or obscures required information where such omission or obscurity would be misleading."
  - R113.1 "A professional accountant shall comply with the principle of professional competence and due care, which requires an accountant to: (a) Attain and maintain professional knowledge and skills at the level required to ensure that a client or employing organization receives competent professional service, based on current technical and professional standards and relevant legislation…"
- **URL:** 2024 Handbook PDF https://ifacweb.blob.core.windows.net/publicfiles/2025-02/2024%20IESBA%20HB_ENG_August%202024_Final.pdf ; landing https://www.ethicsboard.org/

**Attribution / provenance corollary (accountancy):** an unsupported or partial figure is not a reportable figure — the same logic AS 1105 and AS 1215 encode: raw/in-progress material must be distinguished from a completed, supportable deliverable.

## Synthesis — the cross-cutting principle

Every standard above instantiates one governing norm: **the completeness and integrity of the evidentiary record** — the requirement that a record handed over as evidence be whole, closed, and self-disclosing about its own scope, so that an independent consumer can rely on it without having to guess what is missing. In auditing this is the *completeness assertion* and the *sufficient-appropriate evidence / experienced-auditor* documentation tests; in GxP data integrity it is *ALCOA+ "Complete" — the data must be whole, a complete set*; in records management it is ISO 15489 *integrity ("complete and unaltered")*; in forensics it is ISO/IEC 27037's *auditability / reproducibility* and chain-of-custody integrity; and in law it is the *rule of completeness* (FRE 106) against misleading partial records. The specific conduct — bundling a still-running, point-in-time partial output into an evidence set and presenting it as the deliverable — is the textbook failure these frameworks were written to prevent: it corrupts the evidence base precisely because the consumer cannot tell what is missing, which each domain names as a defect of completeness/integrity rather than a mere matter of degree.
