## STRONGEST FLAWS

## Ranked flaws, each verified against the repo record

### 1. The L3 "kill" is refuted by the design's own cited specimen (§2.2 overclaims)
The design cites the soundness.lp ts→id retrofit as "the standing proof this [mutation testing] catches real non-conformance." The instrument's own header (`~/w/vdc/1/epistemic-operator/instruments/soundness.lp`) says the opposite: the non-conformance was caught by a **human fidelity review** ("ID-KEYING RETROFIT (F-D, clingo-fidelity review 2026-07-06)"), and "the banked s10 record has no boundary case, so the model below reproduces BYTE-IDENTICALLY after the retrofit." Every mechanical net the design proposes — banked fixtures, bit-identical reproduction, cross-engine differential — was **provably blind to this defect**: the outputs were byte-identical before and after. The same-second fixture exists only because the review found the divergence first; a mutation only flips a verdict if a fixture already straddles the boundary. **Failure caused:** semantic drift between rule and law that stays off the fixture manifold ships as DERIVED green through citation-diff + mutations + differential. The header "kills L3" launders review-dependence into mechanical assurance — precisely the I9 conflation ("reviewed" masquerading as "proved") the BRIEF exists to forbid. The design's small-print concession ("the diff proves citation presence, not semantic fidelity") is correct and contradicts its own section title.

### 2. The register-diff gate presupposes a machine-readable law register that does not exist, and cannot be derived (§2.2 circularity)
The gate's RED condition — "a ratified consumer-law with no implementing rule" — needs an enumerable set of consumer laws. The actual register (`~/w/vdc/1/epistemic-operator/FINDINGS.md`, 1065 lines) is prose with heterogeneous conventions (`### F1` headings, bold-inline `**F28 —`, grouped `### F47–F51`), and many F-entries are observations, not laws (F4 is "Confidence was well-calibrated"). Deciding which F-entries are laws demanding implementation is judgment. So the gate needs a **hand-curated law list** — exactly the hand-synced middle ADR-0012 P1/P7 and the design's own §2.3 SSOT posture ban. Omitting a newly ratified law from that list is silent non-derivation **at the law layer**, with no oracle — the F49 class reproduced one level up, inside the mechanism built to detect it. Increment 2's acceptance tests only exercise laws already in the register; the failure mode is the law that never enters it. Nothing in the design checks the register's completeness against the rulings stream.

### 3. L7 misdescribes the built system, and independence is spent on the wrong channel (§1 L7 / §2.4, contradicted by ledger_floor.py)
The design asserts "both consume the same `ledger_edb.py` export." False: `ledger_floor.py` computes from the live DB ("input_basis 'live-db'", "This producer shares NO code path with clingo"). What the producers actually share is `ledger_edb.resolve`/`Target` — **target resolution and schema introspection** — and §2.3 *mandates* this sharing ("every derivation resolves its db/schema/session from `ledger_target.py`"). The design says L7 has "no banked specimen yet." Wrong: **F49 is the shared-resolution specimen** — instruments keyed to a prior arm's environment constants, silently not running against the record of record. So the one channel whose failure has a banked total-green-wall specimen is the one channel all three lanes (CTE floor, clingo, proposed raw-audit) are required to share; and the raw-audit lane ("plain SQL against the live schema") is otherwise nearly a duplicate of the floor that already exists. **Failure caused:** a `resolve()` defect (stale registration, wrong schema for a name) feeds every producer the same wrong substrate; AGREE bit-identical; watermark internally consistent; green close over the wrong record. The countermeasure architecture is structurally blind to its own best-evidenced failure mode.

### 4. Totality does not make F49 unconstructible; it relocates it to an unqualified invoker (§2.1, and §5's "exactly one layer" is false arithmetic)
"An output object missing a registered judgment cannot be built" — but F49 was not a malformed output object; it was instruments that **never ran** and a close that read clean anyway. A total map helps only if something independent *requires* the object at close. Python gives runtime constructor checks, not type-level totality; the constructor, register loader, manifest renderer, UNPROVEN-NET display, fixture runner, and watermark stamper are each "a tool whose output is trusted without independent check" — F6/I8's own definition — and none is in the qualification perimeter. Concrete failures: (a) the close never invokes the engine — no object exists to be non-total, increment 1's delete-a-rule-file test never fires; (b) the register file fails to load and a handler defaults to empty — a total map over zero judgments is trivially total and green; (c) a JudgmentSpec is deleted along with its rule — totality holds over the shrunken register (catchable only via flaw-2's non-existent law list). The register — the store enumerating all obligations — is the one mutable, non-append-only artifact in an architecture whose entire creed is append-only. §5's claim that judgment holds "exactly one layer, named, small" undercounts: the judgment-held surface is the whole meta-toolchain.

### 5. DERIVED-EMPTY's grounding witness is program-level, but the lying unit is the rule (§2.1)
In ASP an unmatched predicate is not an error — it is a legal empty relation. Rename one exported fact family (say `amends_scope_len`) or typo a predicate in one rule body: the program still "grounds nonzero rules over a nonzero EDB," the witness passes, that rule silently derives nothing, and its judgment banks as DERIVED(no findings). The A.8 quarantine never fires because other rules keep the model nonempty. **Failure caused:** an export/schema refactor quietly vacates every judgment whose rules consumed the renamed family, close green throughout. The needed witness — per-load-bearing-rule nonzero body instantiation, or clingo's unmatched-atom warnings promoted to RED — is not specified. The "kills L2 structurally" claim has a rule-granular hole.

### 6. §2.5 is internally inconsistent: the Goodhart type constraint forbids the deny pipeline the same section mandates
"A judgment that prices behavior may key **only** on subject-uncontrolled facts" — but a write-time refuse-and-teach trigger fires on the row the subject is authoring: kind, statement, edges are subject-controlled by definition. The e16→e17 specimen pipeline (endorsed one bullet earlier) keyed on subject-authored content. Either "subject-uncontrolled" secretly means the label-vs-structure distinction of the real ratified F26 — in which case it is **not** "checkable from the register," since controlledness is not a syntactic property of a fact family — or the constraint as typed bans every deny surface including the design's flagship. **Failure caused:** implementers either cannot build any trigger, or quietly reinterpret the rule, making the "F26 as a type constraint" guarantee decorative — a lying signature at design scale.

### 7. The bit-identical golden ratchet has no regold protocol, and the record proves regolds will happen (§2.6)
Banked numbers were produced by an instrument later found non-conforming (ts-keyed soundness), and survived the retrofit only by coincidence (id-order and ts-order agree on every banked record). The next fidelity fix that changes a banked number puts the operator in the pilot's own F7 posture: gate red, resolution = edit the golden — and the design specifies no contest record, no approver, no supersedes chain for goldens. **Failure caused:** nothing structurally distinguishes a law-driven regold from evidence-adjustment; the goldens drift under exactly the pressure they exist to resist, with green history.

### 8. Live coverage accounting has a denominator problem (§2.7 × L10)
Coverage = turns examined/degraded/skipped, with the denominator from the acts parser over **discovered** transcripts. Parse-coverage (acts recognized / bytes consumed) is measured over files found; parity against banked transcripts proves the parser reads *old* formats, not that it *finds new files*. **Failure caused:** a vendor update relocates subagent transcripts (Claude Code already makes per-directory project slugs — the CLAUDE.md ephemera clause warns about exactly this dispersal); the parser reports 100% coverage of the main transcript while a whole act stream vanishes; claims-vs-acts judgments quietly weaken; every floor green. The e17 arming-gap also recurs: a never-registered hook banks zero per-turn facts, and noticing requires a turn count independent of both hook and parser — not designed.

### 9. Review-queue debt is unaccounted: the engine cannot lie, but the system silently rots
Flag-never-auto-resolve + a growing judgment register + exactly one reviewer (a solo maintainer, hard budget constraints) = a monotonically growing queue of unadjudicated flags. The design has an F4 analog (per-finding status) but no F5 analog for the review layer — no close line counting open/admitted flags, no aging, no triage. By the BRIEF's own I9 (a Rodin "blue"/admitted obligation is a hazard, never summed away), a close that reads green over hundreds of unread flags is the dressed-up-QED at system level: "the engine ran" substitutes for "the record was reviewed" — the exact substitution I9 forbids. The design polices every conflation except this one.

### Lesser but real
- **Replay-equivalence qualifies the live path only on engine-free trajectories** (§2.7): once live, the engine's flags and teach texts shape the record (L4 concedes the channel "worked, which means it also shapes"); divergence classes arising only under engine-influenced trajectories have no fixture by construction. The gate is sound for what it tests and mute on the deployed regime.
- **Seen-red history is not version-pinned** (§2.6): a rewritten rule under the same judgment id inherits a red-date it never earned — ADR-0011's own point turned against the display layer.
- **Self-conformance block has no second producer** (§2.8): the engine reporting on the engine, rendered by engine code, with I6 ("never adjudicates its own divergence") satisfied only nominally — there is no divergence pair to adjudicate.

## SALVAGE

## What survives the attack, and is load-bearing

- **The threat catalog (L1–L10) itself.** Specimen-grounding is real — I verified F49, F42/F46, F28, F52/F53 exist in FINDINGS.md with the claimed content, and the soundness.lp header confirms the L3 episode. The catalog is the strongest artifact here; my flaws are mostly that the *countermeasures* underdeliver against it, not that the catalog is wrong. Keep it as the organizing register; demote the "kills" headers to "bounds" wherever the mechanism bottoms out in fixture coverage or review.
- **§2.4 (DerivationRecord + watermarks + self-citation currency) genuinely kills L5.** Construction-time resolution of every cited id against the named export is a real structural kill — the one section whose header holds as written, and it is already partly built (marriage A.4/A.5).
- **The closed discharge vocabulary and the DERIVED vs DERIVED-EMPTY distinction.** Right idea, wrong granularity (flaw 5); with per-rule grounding witnesses or warnings-as-RED it becomes what §2.1 claims.
- **UNPROVEN-NET as a display property** is the design's best original move — ADR-0011 applied to the reader. It needs red-history pinned to (judgment, rule-hash, engine-version), then keep it.
- **Parser-as-qualified-tool posture (§2.3)** is correct; it needs a discovery-completeness obligation added, not just parse-parity.
- **The increment ordering** (register + total map before new judgment content) is right per ADR-0012, and the acceptance targets are the right *kind* of test even where incomplete (flaw 4 names the missing negative controls: never-invoked, empty-register, deleted-spec).
- **§2.9's disclaim list** is unusually complete and honest; nothing in it overclaims.
- **The honest-bottom instinct in §5** survives; its arithmetic doesn't. The fix is its own medicine: make the JudgmentSpec register and the goldens append-only ledgered artifacts with contest records (F9-shaped regolds, supersedes-chained specs), put the meta-toolchain (register loader, manifest renderer, close invoker) inside the qualification perimeter with their own negative controls, and add a close line counting unadjudicated flags. None of that requires new theory — it requires applying the design's stated discipline to the three artifacts it exempted: the register, the goldens, and the reviewer's queue.
