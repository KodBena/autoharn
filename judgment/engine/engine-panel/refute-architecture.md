## STRONGEST FLAWS

## 1. The refusal journal cannot be built as stated — and the P5 shadow's "two-sidedness" collapses with it

§3/§6 put the refusal journal "in Postgres": an append-only table populated at T0 with every refusal's attempted payload. But the T0 refusal mechanism is `RAISE EXCEPTION` inside a BEFORE INSERT trigger (`/home/bork/w/vdc/1/epistemic-operator/harness/e17-build/s17-stamp-mechanism.sql:128`, `s17-independence-vocabulary.sql:38,42`). In stock PostgreSQL any journal INSERT made in that transaction **rolls back with the abort** — there are no autonomous transactions. Concrete failure: increment 3's own acceptance test ("replaying the e17 fixture set against an armed scratch kernel populates the journal") produces an empty journal; every P5 reconciliation of journaled refusals is vacuously green over a hole.

Every workaround breaks a stated property: a dblink/postgres_fdw loopback is an undeclared new dependency, a connection per refusal on the subject's latency-budgeted path, and needs its own failure semantics (journal write fails → refuse anyway? then completeness is gone again); client-side journaling by the interception hook misses exactly the writes that bypass the interception — the unstamped-path refusals the threat model cares most about.

The irony is sharp: P4(vii) demands every compiled judgment's "atomicity envelope is part of its semantics, stated, not assumed" — and the design assumes the journal's. Deeper: the journal is the **sole witness** to refusals (refused payloads exist nowhere else by construction), so it is the one artifact in the entire system structurally exempt from the two-producer discipline of §7. "Every actual refusal was journaled" has no oracle. The design claims the journal converts e17's luck-adjacent id-gap visibility into construction; as designed it converts it into an unauditable single point of trust whose capture mechanism does not work.

## 2. The design's own class-A list contains a counterexample to its own criterion — `launder`

§2.1's theorem: class-A membership "is syntactic: every body literal is bounded by ids < E," and class A admits no by-design tier divergence, so "any A-class tier divergence is DIVERGE_DEFECT, mechanically." The membership list includes `launder`. But in the actual built program (`/home/bork/w/vdc/1/claude_harness/experiments/fact-mining/ledger_tnow.lp:66-68`): `launder(E,D,H) :- unsound_derivation(E,D), resolved(E,H), D != H`, where `resolved` consumes `head(D,H)`, and `head` is derived through `not superseded(Y)` — **which the design itself classifies as class C** two paragraphs later. `head(D,·)` mutates whenever a later row supersedes the current head, so `launder(E,D,H)`'s third argument flips after E's insert.

Concrete failure: a T0-cached `launder(E,D,X)` verdict diverges from T2's `launder(E,D,Z)` at the first post-hoc supersession of X — and by the design's own rule this fires DIVERGE_DEFECT, red and non-skippable, on a perfectly healthy record. Either someone reclassifies ad hoc (destroying "never a judgment call") or the strongest guarantee in the system becomes a false-alarm generator.

The structural point is worse than the instance: the classification step is hand-performed. No syntactic checker is built or even proposed as an increment — the registry (increment 1) *stores* a hand-asserted class with a hand-written "justification." The flagship design document, written by the author most steeped in these semantics, got it wrong on its own list. The entire "defect by construction" edifice rests on an unmechanized judgment call that has already failed once, in public, in this document.

## 3. HMAC stamp verification — the one judgment already live at T0 — cannot be shadowed by the stated T2 machinery

The design lists "HMAC stamp verification" as class A and promises T2 recomputes every class-A judgment as the shadow. But T2's producers are clingo over an ids-only EDB (text deliberately excluded, §3 rule 1) and a SQL recursive-CTE floor. clingo cannot compute HMAC-SHA256, and the EDB carries no stamp material. Only the SQL floor could recompute it — with pgcrypto **and the apparatus secret**, which is "provisioned at arm (a fresh random value)" (`s17-stamp-mechanism.sql:47-52`) and whose retention is stated nowhere. Concrete failures:
- The "two independent producers" discipline degenerates to at most one (and possibly zero) producers for exactly the judgment the whole promotion pipeline was built from. tier_consistency for the e17 gate is not derivable as specified.
- If the secret is retained for the BRIEF's I11 horizon ("re-runnable by an inspector decades later"), the tripwire's own pre-registered limit ("the secret is hunt-able") widens from a session to forever, and handing it to an inspector destroys the secrecy that makes the stamp mean anything. If it is not retained, `stamp_verified` is trust-me forever — a stored verdict with no re-derivation path, precisely the shape the two-theory law exists to forbid.
- The freshness window (`skew > 300`, keyed on `now()` at evaluation, line 82-84) makes the refusal verdict a function of an instant that, for refused writes, is recorded only in the journal — see flaw 1.

## 4. The codegen target state (§6/increment 7) and the verification discipline (§7) are mutually destructive, and the project's own record already proved the hazard

§7's argument: "three encodings agreeing is stronger evidence than two." §6's named target: "one authority module generating all three encodings." After adoption, tier_consistency and the T2-internal differential verify only that one generator is deterministic — a textbook common-mode channel, exactly what the BRIEF's F15/ARP4761 channel-independence record and I6 exist to forbid in the systems this apparatus is supposed to audit. This is not hypothetical for this codebase: `ledger_tnow.lp:85-91` (the F-A note) documents that **both producers shared a weaker `answered` rule and "the differential was structurally BLIND to it (twins agree; agreement is not fidelity)"** — a live instance of shared-encoding blindness caught only by an outside fidelity review. The design cites ADR-0012 P7 for generate-from-one-source and never confronts that its two disciplines cancel; no "generate at most N−1 of the N encodings" rule exists. Concrete failure: post-increment-7, a generator bug refuses honest writes at T0, reproduces at T2, and every close line agrees — green across the board while the record thins (the F26/F29 detonation the design itself names).

## 5. pg_stat_statements cannot deliver the latency_budget close line as specified

§5 protocol item 4 claims the in-production evidence "compares the session's INSERT timing distribution against the armed baseline" via pg_stat_statements, calling per-row timing unnecessary because "stock Postgres gives the statement-level view free." pg_stat_statements gives cumulative mean/min/max/stddev per query fingerprint per (userid, dbid) — **no percentiles, no per-session attribution, counters shared across everything on the harness DB** (which also hosts the perf ledger, toolset registry, and work log). The design's own stated hazard currency is tail behavior — denial-by-friction lives at p95/p99 — and tails are invisible in retained moments; a reset discipline sharp enough to isolate one session destroys the other stores' statistics. Concrete failure: the exact drift the close line exists to catch (a trigger whose p99 quietly triples under load) passes green because the mean barely moves and half the samples belong to another session.

## SALVAGE

## What survives, and is load-bearing

- **The prefix-determinedness insight itself.** Given append-only + strictly-backward edges + id-is-order (all genuinely settled law in this record), a correctly-classified prefix-determined judgment really is the same function at every tier, and tier divergence on it really is a defect. This is the document's core theorem and it holds — the attack in flaw 2 is on the unmechanized *classification*, not the theorem. The repair is even cheap: the syntactic criterion is checkable over `.lp` rule text; build the checker into increment 1 and the theorem regains its teeth.
- **Class-B monotone caches under rebuild-and-diff.** Append-only caches for monotone truths with a mandatory bit-identical rebuild close line is sound, cheap, and honestly reconciled with the T_now-never-stored law (§4's argument for why the caches are memoization, not a second truth, is actually persuasive — no class-C fact materialized, no subject grants, standing audit).
- **The P3 verified-claim vs. priced-label criterion.** The e9/e17 contrast (a gate must verify the claim against an invariant witness the writer doesn't control, never price the label) is the best single design rule in the document, grounded in two banked worked instances (`s17-independence-vocabulary.sql` is exactly that shape, and it worked live). It should simply stop being advertised as "mechanical."
- **The tier vocabulary and authority rule.** T2-authoritative with lower tiers as shadows, T1 never denies, C/C_t/D constitutionally ineligible for refusal — the constitution is right even where the flagship trigger needs its freshness check re-derived against it (flaw 3's window point). Closed verdict vocabulary reuse rather than proliferation is correct.
- **The refusal to invent latency numbers and scaling claims** (§5, §8, §11). Currencies-protocol-enforcement with budgets ratified from banked measurements is the honest shape; the microbench arm and the arm-time budget check are buildable as stated. Only the pg_stat_statements production leg (flaw 5) is oversold.
- **The judgment registry** (increment 1). The prose-scattered tier assignments are a real ADR-0012 cancer-G instance; a typed single home is the right fix — and it is also where flaws 2 and 6's repairs live.
- **The T0-freshness of the record's structural laws.** The claim that T0 mechanization outperformed prose primes is consistent with the e17 record I inspected; the append-only/MAC lesson (F15-pilot) is correctly carried.
- **The honest-unknowns register (§11) and the increment sequencing with per-increment acceptance targets** — the discipline is real, even though increments 3 and 4's specific acceptance criteria are broken as written (flaws 1, smaller-items) and must be redrafted around a workable journaling mechanism.
- **§8's grounding-hygiene *direction*** (domain-restrict the `defeated_asof` idiom, bank grounding stats, pre-register the growth response, session as the grounding unit) — correct and cheap; only the complexity arithmetic needs fixing before it is banked.
- **Increment 8's acts_parity posture**: nothing consumes the live acts stream before whole-run parity against the transcript parse — the right gate, correctly scoped inside the standing threat model.

Net: the skeleton — ladder, classification theorem, shadows, promotion-with-ratified-law — is worth building. What must change before increment 3: a workable refusal-capture mechanism with a stated atomicity story and a second witness; a mechanical class checker; a stated secret-retention/re-derivation policy for the crypto judgment; an explicit independence-preservation rule bounding the codegen ambition; and the marriage-§8 divergence surfaced to the maintainer as a divergence.
