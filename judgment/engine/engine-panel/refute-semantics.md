## STRONGEST FLAWS

## 1. "Silence is unrepresentable" is false against the design's own closed enums — the F49 hole is re-opened at exactly the schedule the design exists to serve

§1 claims, as a type-level property: "Every family has a QUARANTINED/REQUIRED-ABSENT-shaped member for 'the derivation did not run.'" The §2 table contradicts it: families **B, C, D, E, F, H have no such member** in their closed verdict sets ({CONTEMPORANEOUS, BATCHED, GAP}, {IN-FORCE, DEFEATED, CLAUSE-DEFEATED, ANSWERED, EXPIRED}, etc.). Only A and G carry one. Since the enums are *closed and generated from the authority module* (§4.1), a builder implementing the table as written cannot represent a per-family non-run. The design's fallback — Family G's empty-model quarantine and the close manifest — is **close-time only** (G's own mode ceiling says so). At turn-time nothing enumerates expected derivations, and increment 4's acceptance target says outright "**daemons-down is a no-op**." Concrete failure: the turn-time hook's clingo call dies mid-session (the exact A.8 empty-model class), the subject works for hours on an uncurrent citation, no judgment row exists, nothing is red until close — the silent-non-run class F49 names (`~/w/vdc/1/epistemic-operator/FINDINGS.md` line 942: "none of the three mandatory §H close lines ran... and no artifact flagged the non-run"), reproduced at the schedule where the design's whole live-value proposition sits. Either §1's bullet is false, or the §2 enums and increment-4 acceptance are wrong; all three cannot stand.

## 2. The frontier is an incomplete replay index: wall-clock `now` is a hidden EDB input, and it breaks both the replay guarantee and increment 2's acceptance target

§3.2's law: a banked judgment pins its frontier and "replay at that frontier must reproduce it bit-identically." But Family B expiry and Family C "assumption-expiry propagation" are functions of **(frontier, now)**, not frontier: `ledger_assumes.lp` (`expired_temporal(A) :- valid_until(A,B), now(N), N > B`, with `now/1` explicitly "injected at run") makes IN-BOUND/EXPIRED flip on an unchanged record as the clock advances. The §1 Judgment type has no clock component and the design never states `now` is pinned in the DerivationRecord/EDB hash. Three concrete failures: (a) replay of an IN-BOUND judgment after the bound derives EXPIRED → the §5 drift guard ("any change to a banked fixture's output without a law amendment is DRIFT → red") fires a false red on a program nobody changed; (b) increment 2's acceptance "re-running on an unchanged record inserts zero rows" is **jointly unsatisfiable** with expiry semantics — a lapsed `valid_until` on an unchanged record must yield a new EXPIRED judgment, which is not a re-observation of any stored finding; either the idempotency gate suppresses it (an expired assumption stays IN-BOUND in the store — a safety miss, the precise I7 hazard the BRIEF calls the dressed-up-QED) or the acceptance test fails; (c) the table's "monotone" column is false for Family B under an advancing clock, and monotonicity is what justifies its SQL assignment. The fix is small (export `now(T)` hash-covered; add time to the identity key for time-indexed families) — but as written the guarantee does not hold.

## 3. Cross-stream precedence has no sound order under the design's own axioms — yet the BRIEF's central deontic check (G5, approval-precedes-implementation) is placed in Family E and declared "BRIEF-sized now"

Ratified law (§3.2): id is the record order; ts's only sanctioned roles are comparisons against an external clock, "**neither an ordering within the record**." Ids are per-stream; §8.3 concedes cross-stream frontier semantics is an open question and confines Family F to close-time for that reason. But §2.1 assigns **G5** — "approver identity + timestamp predating implementation" (BRIEF §3.1), the load-bearing obligation of IEC 62304/820.30/SOX — to Family E, and §8.6 claims Family E's vocabulary "is BRIEF-sized now." Deriving approval-precedes-implementation requires ordering a *ledger row* (approval) against an *acts-stream event* (implementation): no shared id order exists, and ts-ordering is banned — with cause, since the record contains same-second neighbours (consult 17 §5.3, A.3). So the G5 class is underivable at **any** schedule under the design's own axioms, and the design nowhere flags it as blocked on §8.3. Concrete failure: the builder either smuggles ts-ordering back in (unsound on the same-second cases the law was written for) or ships G5 as silently absent while the registry says Family E covers it — scope misdisclosure, the I12 failure the design lectures against. Same inconsistency one row up: Family B's contemporaneity gap-detection is by definition a ledger-vs-acts join, yet B is scheduled turn-time with no frontier-pair protection while F is deferred to close-time for exactly that hazard.

## 4. "One semantics, three schedules" is false as stated: write-time is delta-local evaluation of nonmonotone programs, and the promotion criteria measure budget, not verdict-equivalence

§6's headline: "the same program text evaluated at three trigger points with different EDB frontiers." But write-time EDB scope is "delta + the bounded closure it touches" (§6, §8.1). For the nonmonotone families (C/D/E — E's locality is declared **global**), default negation over a truncated fact base is not conservative in either direction: a defeater outside the bound yields a wrongful REFUSE (refusing an honest act, with a frozen taught text asserting a verdict the same program at turn-time retracts) or a missed defeat yields a wrongful pass. Promotion criterion (i) requires only that the judgment is "computable on the delta plus a bounded closure **inside the trigger budget**" — a latency condition, not an equivalence condition. The one proven write-time specimen (F53 stamp-distinctness, e17) is trivially row-local; the design generalizes the ladder past it without ever requiring *delta-closure verdict ≡ full-frontier verdict, proven per judgment*. Concrete failure: by the design's own e17/F26 logic, an illegible or wrong refusal converts subjects into evasion — a write-time refusal later contradicted by the engine's own turn-time derivation is the most corrosive artifact this architecture can emit, and nothing in §6 forbids building one.

## 6. Family C's closed enum cannot represent the F44 aspectual state its own record made famous — and the EDB withholds the subject that would resolve it

{IN-FORCE, DEFEATED, CLAUSE-DEFEATED, ANSWERED, EXPIRED} as one verdict per subject collides with F44's triple: a row in force **whose load-bearing clause is defeated** — two simultaneously true things about one row, which is why the FDE lens exists. Concrete failure: dedup keyed on (family, subject, frontier) drops one half of the F44 triple — the boolean lie the whole FDE section exists to prevent — or the store admits per-subject contradictions with no declared semantics.

## SALVAGE

Every component is dictated by a paid-for negative specimen I verified in the record (F49 silent non-run, F51's un-adjudicable alias flags, the pre-A.4 unreplayable posture). It needs a sixth component (evaluation clock, per flaw 2) — an amendment, not a refutation.
- **Per-family closed verdict enums with one declared aggregation point at close.** The I9/no-global-truth-lattice argument is sound and matches the CellLedger specimen (chocofarm ADR-0000 Specimen 2 — the design miscites it as ADR-0008, but ADR-0000 itself cross-files it there, so the cite resolves).
- **The deontic architecture** (§3.3): no modal operators, obligation as recorded other-assigned fact, the engine deriving only discharge status, violation as a routed flag. This is the strongest section — every plank is grounded in a verified specimen (F26 three instances, F28 executable launder proof, e17's refuse-and-teach arc, which I confirmed in consult 35: row-12 stub, stamp-distinct rows 17/18, the live `enacts=[15]` FK refusal). Calling it "the Anderson reduction" is a misnomer (no modal necessity anywhere), but the architecture decision the name decorates is right.
- **The quotational stance on paraconsistency** (§3.4): atoms are facts *about* assertions, so record-contradiction is data, not theory-inconsistency — this genuinely dissolves the explosion problem at the kernel, and the report-side-only FDE confinement follows correctly.
- **id-not-ts within a stream, the two-theory split, and the probe/judgment lane separation** (§3.1–3.2). The choice-rules-never-feed-judgments rule is a real category-error guard and `core_a.lp`'s own header already practices it. - **Law-as-data with ratification-depth marking** (§4.1, §4.4): the authority-module/generated-enum/parity discipline is the kb_ledger idiom already working, and marking each law {RATIFIED-RULING, FINDINGS-ENTRY, BRIEF-INVARIANT, ADR} is exactly the honest answer to the census problem — the flaw (5) is sequencing the constructor-refusal before the census, not the mechanism.
- **The admission protocol** (§5: law + banked specimen + flipping mutation + justified engine assignment + declared mode ceiling) and **Family H as a first-class non-derivability register**. Both are the project's own hard-won discipline pointed at the engine itself; nothing in my attack touches them.

A design that hands its critic the knife is doing something right; the failure is that the vocabulary tables and acceptance targets don't yet honor the limits its own §8 concedes.
