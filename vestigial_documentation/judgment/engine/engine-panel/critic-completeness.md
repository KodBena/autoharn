# Completeness critique — what the four-lens panel is missing

Basis: both source documents read in full this invocation (`experiments/fact-mining/docs/LEDGER-LOGIC-MARRIAGE.md` incl. Appendix A; `experiments/fact-mining/docs/safety-critical-logging-standards/BRIEF.md`), plus the four designs and refutations as given. I do not re-argue any refutation; everything below is absent from all eight documents.

## 1. The panel as a whole violates ADR-0012 P1 — four authority modules, no owner of the union

Each lens mints its own single-source-of-truth registry: `judgment_authority.py` (semantics §4), the "judgment registry" (architecture §3/inc.1), the expectations-ledger authority (evaluation §2), `judgment_register.py` (adversarial §2.1). All four carry overlapping content (families, verdict enums, law citations, fixtures, mode/tier placement, exclusions) under four schemas. Built as written, the project gets four hand-synced homes for one truth — the exact cancer each design individually condemns. Same disease in the vocabulary: four closed verdict vocabularies that overlap without mapping (semantics' per-family enums; architecture's tier-line verdicts; evaluation's {REPRODUCED, DERIVED, …, SUPERSEDED}; adversarial's DischargeStatus), with QUARANTINED appearing in all four with subtly different meanings — and semantics' own law says shared spelling with divergent meaning is how drift starts. Likewise four names for one concept: frontier / watermark / as_of / prefix ≤ W. And four increment-1s each claiming build priority, with no merged build order across designs. **Nobody owns the unification, and it is the first thing a builder needs.**

## 2. Silent cross-design contradictions

- **What runs at turn-time.** Semantics §6's headline is "the *same program text* evaluated at three trigger points." Architecture §3 states "clingo is *not* on the T1 hot path initially… C-class evaluation over cached B-facts is joins and complements" — i.e. turn-time nonmonotone judgments are computed by a **third, hand-written SQL-over-caches encoding** that semantics' one-semantics claim rules out, and that neither design's two-writers/differential accounting counts. hook_fidelity (architecture §7) reconciles T1 against T2 at close, but nobody states that T1 is a distinct encoding requiring its own mutation set and registry row.
- **Whether the engine ever feeds a deny surface.** Evaluation M3: "the engine layer never becomes a deny surface in this design," F26/F28 cited as law. Semantics §6 (promotion ladder, increment 6) and architecture P0–P5 exist precisely to compile engine judgments into refusing triggers. The architecture refutation surfaced the marriage-§8 divergence for architecture alone; the three-way disagreement across designs on the single most safety-relevant boundary is surfaced nowhere, and the maintainer ruling it needs is requested by nobody.
- **Stored judgments vs. never-stored T_now.** Architecture §12: "no class-C validity fact is ever materialized." Semantics increment 2: a judgment store persisting Family C verdicts (IN-FORCE at frontier N) as rows. There is a plausible reconciliation (a frontier-pinned verdict is a historical fact about a prefix), but neither design argues it, and a consumer reading the store's IN-FORCE rows as current is the e9 sin re-enabled by furniture the architecture design says must not exist.
- **Three incompatible FindingIdentity schemes.** Semantics keys storage on (family, subject, frontier)+law; evaluation content-hashes (substrate, family, row ids) with no as-of; the marriage §6.1/§6.2 donation is the unordered claim-pair plus a session-hash for cross-session accumulation. Hook dedup, hook_fidelity reconciliation, and eval discharge each depend on identity, and as specified they cannot key against each other's stores.
- **Family F promotion preconditions diverge — and the solution goes unnoticed.** Semantics blocks cross-stream judgments on the unsolved frontier-pair semantics (§8.3); architecture increment 8 promotes acts-joined judgments to T1 gated only on acts_parity. Neither cites the other's precondition. More importantly: architecture's `acts_live` — an apparatus-owned Postgres table in the same DB — could give ledger and acts writes **one shared id sequence**, which would dissolve semantics' cross-stream ordering problem (and the G5 approval-precedes-implementation derivability gap) by construction. No document connects the two.
- Minor: budget ratification authority (evaluation derives budgets from measurements; architecture requires a maintainer ruling per budget) and aggregation surfaces (semantics says cross-family aggregation happens "in exactly one place, the close manifest"; evaluation's discharge table and adversarial's self-conformance block are two more).

## 3. Interactions between lenses that nobody owns

- **The adjudication feedback loop.** Every design routes flags to "the review queue" and semantics states rulings "flow back as appended facts the engine then consumes." Nobody specifies *where* an adjudication lands (ledger row? acts.ruling? a third table?), what id/frontier it carries, or the stability of the loop engine-output → queue → ruling → EDB → engine-input (a ruling can flip verdicts that generated other pending flags — re-derivation churn semantics are unstated). The project already has a designed adjudication surface (`experiments/adjudicate/`, schema-first HITL) that no design mentions or wires to.
- **Session-scoped engine vs. lifecycle-scoped obligations.** Architecture's partitioning law: "T_now judgments never span sessions; the engine grounds a session, not a life." The BRIEF's obligations are lifecycle-scoped: a G5 approval and its implementation, or an obligation and its discharge, routinely land in different sessions. Family E discharge closure confined to one session cannot see a discharge that arrives next session; the KB accumulation layer stores *findings*, not open-obligation state. No lens designs cross-session obligation carry-over, and the BRIEF (authoritative on scope, per standing rule) plainly demands it.
- **The law register rides the acts stream.** Laws are `acts.ruling` rows; semantics' STALE-LAW is Family C closure *over the law register* — a cross-stream judgment, inheriting exactly the unsolved §8.3 frontier semantics. Also unasked: **law retroactivity** — when a new law ratifies (F53), are prior records re-judged under it, and with what verdict (they cannot satisfy stamp-distinctness that predates stamps)? Capability manifests cover absent *fact families*, not absent *law eras*; no design has a law-epoch concept.
- **Specimen contamination after arming.** Once the engine runs live, every subsequent organic specimen is produced by a subject-plus-engine system, while the entire fixture corpus was banked from subject-only runs. Architecture's P0 ("observed" organically) and evaluation's Tier-2 both consume future specimens with no field recording the apparatus configuration under which the specimen arose. No fixture schema in any design carries armed-state provenance; nobody owns re-baselining.
- **The union of subject-facing prime surface.** T0 teach texts (architecture), turn-time injected findings (semantics), STALE presentations (evaluation), L3′ interrogation payloads — each lens F26-audits its own channel; no one audits the *union* as one vocabulary-shaping surface per the e12 passive-prime discipline.
- **One target-resolution channel under all producers.** Every design mandates resolution through the `ledger_target` SSOT (correctly, individually). Collectively, all producers at all tiers plus the eval harness share the one channel whose failure mode has the project's best-documented green-wall specimen (F49). Individually declared; collectively unmitigated — no design gives any lane an independent way to notice it is pointed at the wrong substrate.
- **Concurrency.** Multiple sessions write the shared harness DB; AFTER-trigger cache maintenance, hook cursors, and "frontier = max id at export" all silently assume a single writer. Isolation semantics for cache maintenance under concurrent inserts, and two hooks racing on one findings store, are unexamined by every lens.
- **The panel's own common mode.** All four designs *and* all four refutations were produced by claude-fable-5, and each refuter read only its own lens — so cross-design contradictions (§2 above) were structurally undetectable by the process, and every model-level blind spot is shared between design and attack. That is the BRIEF's F15/I6 channel-independence obligation applied to the design process itself, and no document discloses it.

## 4. Source-document constraints nobody honored

- **F13 / I11 — decades-scale re-runnability and expertise decay.** The BRIEF's spine lesson (Maeslantkering: 30 years on, Z/Promela expertise loss impedes maintenance) obliges a retention/legibility *plan*. No design states retention periods or archival for the judgment store, DerivationRecords, refusal journal, expectations ledger, or — critically — **archived solver versions** (can clingo-of-2026 be re-run by an inspector in 2046?). The HMAC-secret retention question (raised in one refutation) is one corner of this wholly unowned obligation.
- **G12 — retention/access events.** No design records who reads/exports the judgment store or grants an auditor access. Zero coverage.
- **G14 — explicit security scope declaration.** The engine adds attack-relevant surfaces (the apparatus secret, the refusal journal, engine_cache, the hook process, acts_live). The BRIEF's rule is that security is either assessed or *explicitly declared out of scope — silence is not acceptable*. All four designs are silent. (The standing threat-model scope memory justifies a narrow scope; it does not waive the recorded declaration.)
- **F10 — structured soundness basis.** The engine substitutes mechanical derivation for human review of the record. F10 requires, when a formal method replaces another verification means: the abstraction gap named and an argument that it is **conservative**. The ids-not-text EDB is exactly such an abstraction (judgments run over `amends_scope_len`, never the quotation; text stays SQL-side), and no lens argues in which direction that gap errs. No engine-side F10(a)–(e) entry exists anywhere.
- **F12 / GSN — the connecting argument.** All four designs produce evidence (discharge tables, conformance blocks, differentials); none produces the assurance-case *argument structure* connecting "these lines are green" to the claim "the record is honest" — 15026-2's point that "proved in clingo" is a talisman without the argument. The engine's own assurance case is unowned.
- **Part 11's trusted clock.** C_t judgments and stamp freshness key on `now()`; the BRIEF requires "a clock the user cannot alter." No design states the time source or its trust basis — pinning `now` (the refutations' fix) makes it replayable, not trustworthy.
- **I1/Original for the acts substrate.** Vendor transcripts live in prunable `~/.claude`/`/tmp` until the post-hoc ephemera snapshot; between act and persistence the acts stream's originality/contemporaneity rests on unchecksummed volatile files. acts_live fixes this forward; nobody flags the standing window for the historical corpus every fixture rests on.
- **Marriage §10's solver-file hazard vs. the build plans.** The marriage pre-registers that solver-file text is a suspected degradation trigger for the very model class expected to do the hard work — and every design's build plan requires *authoring* many new `.lp`/law-constant files. No design carries the operational constraint of who safely writes and reviews them.
- **Marriage §7's dogfood target** ("this repo's own sessions, per the standing maintainer note") appears in no milestone plan — evaluation shadows e18 only.
- **Bus factor.** The BRIEF's I11 inspector is "not present at creation"; the apparatus has one maintainer, one secret, one adjudicator. No lens asks what the engine's evidence is worth, or how the queue drains, if that one person is unavailable — the operational-continuity analog of the expertise-decay lesson.

## 5. Questions no lens asked (residue)

- May the subject query the judgment store / its own flags, and is that read surface a prime? (Injection is designed; pull access is not.)
- What happens to the registry when the NLP lane matures and a second substrate joins behind the seam (marriage §8) — is any judgment family substrate-generic?
- What refuses or quarantines a *failed engine write* (journal insert error, store constraint violation, disk full)? The engine's own write path sits behind no write boundary.
- Schema-lineage evolution: a judgment family's meaning across s13 → s17 → DTO-era lineages is handled per-target by capability manifests, but no design states whether one family id may mean different closures on different lineages without that being drift.

The highest-leverage absences, if I had to rank three: the unification vacuum (§1 — four registries, four vocabularies, no merged build order), the cross-session obligation gap (§3 — the engine as designed cannot represent the BRIEF's central obligation shape), and the deny-surface three-way disagreement (§2 — needs one maintainer ruling before any increment 1 is cut).
