`forbidden: <task-shape>` is a fourth TIER value on the `resource:` declaration grammar,
completing the MAY/SHOULD/MUST/MUST-NOT deontic register the existing three tiers
(available/blessed/mandated) already three-quarters covered. Two mechanisms changed:

  1. `bootstrap/templates/led.tmpl`'s `resource:` intake validator now ACCEPTS a well-formed
     `forbidden: <task-shape>` TIER (same whitespace-normalized-copy / byte-exact-persisted /
     refuse-before-write discipline the existing three tiers already carry) and REFUSES a
     malformed one (bare `forbidden` with no colon; `forbidden:` with an empty task-shape).
  2. `bootstrap/templates/pickup.tmpl`'s RESOURCES section now sorts a `forbidden` entry AHEAD
     of a `mandated` entry (ORCH-SPEC-RESOURCE-ACCOUNTING.md §3: "a prohibition outranks a
     mandate for a reader's attention").

Nothing existing is relaxed: `available`/`blessed`/`mandated` accept exactly what they accepted
before this change (seen-red/resource-intake-validation/ continues to prove that, unchanged).
This fixture proves only the ADDITION.
