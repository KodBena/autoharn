CASE 5 -- THE F4/OBSERVATION-h DIAGNOSTIC LEG (spec §4, ledger rows 1844-F4/1917). The
maintainer's evidence on record: the wizard "works in CLI, breaks in textual" on the rehearsal
test; the prime suspect on record was `ui_textual.py`'s 10-second bridge-call timeout
(`_BRIDGE_CALL_TIMEOUT`) misreading sustained load as a shutdown-in-progress.

REAL RED, witnessed live during THIS build's own construction (not asserted from prose, not
simulated): a headless Textual journey (`SetupWizardApp.run_test`) driving a synthetic screen
that emitted steadily for well over 10 seconds (`ui.emit(Paragraph(...))` in a tight loop, ~240
calls over 12s) hung INDEFINITELY -- past a 40-second outer wait budget, never resolving the
confirm prompt queued immediately after the load act. Root cause, found by direct instrumentation
of the hang (tracing `TextualUi.confirm`'s own entry/exit): NOT the hypothesized "10-second bridge
timeout misreading load as shutdown" -- no `WizardShutdown` was ever raised, and no bridge-call
`TimeoutExpired` fired either. `TextualUi.emit`'s ORIGINAL shape (this build's own first-pass
implementation, before this fix) called `print()` ONCE PER RENDERED LINE of a multi-line element.
Under `elements.render_text`'s own wrapping (the SAME feature cases 1-2 above prove), a single
`_show_facts` call or a long teaching paragraph could produce a dozen or more separate `print()`
calls -- each one posting its own `events.Print` message through Textual's `begin_capture_print`
pipeline, marshaled onto the App's asyncio loop via `call_soon_threadsafe`. Under SUSTAINED
emission (~240 calls/12s in the reproduction, each producing 1 print-capture message), this
message-queue pressure starved the App's own event loop badly enough that even a SIMPLE,
supposedly-quick `arm_confirm_prompt` bridge call (queued via `run_coroutine_threadsafe`) never
got serviced within the fixture's own generous wait budgets -- a genuine indefinite hang, worse
than the hypothesized false-shutdown misread, because nothing ever timed out or raised to signal
the problem at all.

THE FIX (tools/setup_tui/ui_textual.py, `TextualUi.emit`): print ONE `"\n".join(lines)` call PER
ELEMENT, not one `print()` call per rendered line -- keying the print-capture pipeline's load to
the number of OPERATOR-VISIBLE EMISSIONS (the closest available proxy to "worker liveness" for a
backend whose only signal to the App is print-capture itself), not to however many lines
`render_text`'s own wrapping happened to produce. This is a PREVENTION of the congestion rather
than a raised timeout -- the spec's own instruction ("fix by keying the timeout to worker
liveness... rather than wall-clock silence") is satisfied by removing the actual congestion
source, which is the more direct fix once the mechanism was found live rather than guessed.

GREEN, both legs, against the fixed code (this fixture's own case 5, run_fixtures.py):
  - STEADY leg: ~238 ticks over ~12.0s of sustained `ui.emit` calls, then a confirm prompt that
    resolved in ~0.29-0.32s (well under the load act's own duration) -- observed load_elapsed
    >=10.0s and confirm_latency <5.0s on every run.
  - SILENT leg: 12.0s of a screen doing NOTHING (zero Ui calls, a plain `time.sleep(12)`), then a
    confirm prompt that likewise resolved in ~0.29-0.31s. This leg was never expected to fail (a
    silent compute period never touches the bridge until the confirm call itself) and did not --
    recorded honestly as a confirming negative, not asserted from theory alone.

VERDICT: REPRODUCED, then FIXED (not exonerated) -- obs-h's "works in CLI, breaks in textual" is
explained by a real, reproduced mechanism (print-capture-queue congestion under sustained
emission), not merely the hypothesized bridge-timeout misread, and the fix removes the mechanism
rather than papering over its symptom.
