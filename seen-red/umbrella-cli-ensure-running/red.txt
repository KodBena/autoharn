Witnessed 2026-07-23: seen-red/umbrella-cli-ensure-running/run_fixtures.py caught a REAL
TOCTOU race in the first draft of libexec/autoharn-service's bind-as-lock adoption logic (design/
FABLE-AUTOHARN-UMBRELLA-CLI-SPEC.md §2's "the port bind IS the lock ... a loser detects the lost
bind ... and ADOPTS the winner").

THE DEFECT: the first draft declared ownership of a spawned child as soon as `_probe(url)`
succeeded AND `proc.poll() is None` -- but a LOSING child can still be alive (mid-import,
mid-startup, about to crash on its own failed `bind()`) at the exact instant a WINNING sibling's
bind makes the boundary reachable. `proc.poll() is None` at that instant does not yet prove this
invocation's own child is the one actually holding the port.

REPRODUCTION (case d, two concurrent `autoharn service start` invocations against the same down
scratch port, before the fix):

$ python3 seen-red/umbrella-cli-ensure-running/run_fixtures.py
a-status-unreachable: PASS (RED case: exit 1, UNREACHABLE)
b-cold-spawn: PASS (one stderr notice, reachable after spawn, pidfile written)
c-warm-adopt-silent: PASS (no new spawn, exit 0)
d-race-two-invocations: FAIL -- a(exit=0, out="autoharn service: reachable at
http://127.0.0.1:56321 (this invocation's own spawn, pid 438251).\n") b(exit=0, out="autoharn
service: reachable at http://127.0.0.1:56321 (this invocation's own spawn, pid 438252).\n")
e-explicit-stop: FAIL -- exit 0, stdout=''
f-stop-without-pidfile-refuses: PASS (RED case: exit 1, teaches, never guesses)

AT LEAST ONE CASE FAILED

BOTH invocations claimed ownership of the winning spawn (only one, pid 438251, was actually
alive per `ps`/`ss` -- the other, 438252, had lost the real OS-level bind race and was in the
process of exiting when B's own poll() ran). The pidfile was overwritten by whichever invocation
wrote it last, which could be the LOSER's own (already-dying) pid -- exactly the "claim a pid we
do not actually own" hazard case f's own explicit-stop machinery depends on never happening.

THE FIX: a 0.5s grace-delay re-check of `proc.poll()` after the reachability probe succeeds,
before declaring ownership and writing the pidfile (libexec/autoharn-service, the
`# TOCTOU guard` comment). A false negative (this invocation actually won but reports "adopted")
is harmless per row 1165 ("someone-else-started-it is a success case" regardless of who it truly
was); a false positive (claiming a pid this invocation does not own) is the hazard closed here.

Post-fix, the same case (and the full six-case run) passes reliably across repeated runs -- see
the umbrella-CLI build's completion report for the green transcript.
