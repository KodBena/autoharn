Witnessed 2026-07-23: seen-red/umbrella-cli-ensure-running/run_fixtures.py caught a REAL
TOCTOU race in the first draft of libexec/autoharn-service's bind-as-lock adoption logic (design/
FABLE-AUTOHARN-UMBRELLA-CLI-SPEC.md §2's "the port bind IS the lock ... a loser detects the lost
bind ... and ADOPTS the winner").

THE DEFECT: the first draft declared ownership of a spawned child as soon as `_probe(url)`
succeeded AND `proc.poll() is None` -- but a LOSING child can still be alive (mid-import,
mid-startup, about to crash on its own failed `bind()`) at the exact instant a WINNING sibling's
bind makes the boundary reachable. `proc.poll() is None` at that instant does not yet prove this
invocation's own child is the one actually holding the port.

REPRODUCTION (case d, two concurrent `autoharn service start` invocations against the same down
scratch port, before the fix):

$ python3 seen-red/umbrella-cli-ensure-running/run_fixtures.py
a-status-unreachable: PASS (RED case: exit 1, UNREACHABLE)
b-cold-spawn: PASS (one stderr notice, reachable after spawn, pidfile written)
c-warm-adopt-silent: PASS (no new spawn, exit 0)
d-race-two-invocations: FAIL -- a(exit=0, out="autoharn service: reachable at
http://127.0.0.1:56321 (this invocation's own spawn, pid 438251).\n") b(exit=0, out="autoharn
service: reachable at http://127.0.0.1:56321 (this invocation's own spawn, pid 438252).\n")
e-explicit-stop: FAIL -- exit 0, stdout=''
f-stop-without-pidfile-refuses: PASS (RED case: exit 1, teaches, never guesses)

AT LEAST ONE CASE FAILED

BOTH invocations claimed ownership of the winning spawn (only one, pid 438251, was actually
alive per `ps`/`ss` -- the other, 438252, had lost the real OS-level bind race and was in the
process of exiting when B's own poll() ran). The pidfile was overwritten by whichever invocation
wrote it last, which could be the LOSER's own (already-dying) pid -- exactly the "claim a pid we
do not actually own" hazard case f's own explicit-stop machinery depends on never happening.

THE FIX: a 0.5s grace-delay re-check of `proc.poll()` after the reachability probe succeeds,
before declaring ownership and writing the pidfile (libexec/autoharn-service, the
`# TOCTOU guard` comment). A false negative (this invocation actually won but reports "adopted")
is harmless per row 1165 ("someone-else-started-it is a success case" regardless of who it truly
was); a false positive (claiming a pid this invocation does not own) is the hazard closed here.

Post-fix, the same case (and the full six-case run) passes reliably across repeated runs -- see
the umbrella-CLI build's completion report for the green transcript.

ROUND-1 REVIEW (2026-07-23), SEVERE-2: the 0.5s grace-delay fix above is ITSELF a timing
assumption, not a structural check -- a fresh-context reviewer named the residual hazard: a
losing child can still be alive (mid-import, about to crash on its own failed bind) past the
grace window under different scheduling, and the winner's identity was never independently
confirmed against the OS's own view of who holds the port.

THE STRUCTURAL FIX: `serving/boundary_service.py` now binds its OWN listen socket itself,
synchronously, in `main()`, BEFORE uvicorn/ASGI ever start, and writes `<world>/.autoharn-
service.pid` immediately after that succeeds -- `libexec/autoharn-service` passes `--pidfile
<path>` to every child it spawns. `seen-red/umbrella-cli-ensure-running/run_fixtures.py` case g
("g-race-structural-winner") was added to prove this structurally: after the race, THREE
independent sources of truth (the `/proc` process census, `ss`'s own socket-table view of who
holds the port, and the pidfile) must all name the same single pid.

REPRODUCING THE OLD (grace-sleep) DEFECT AGAINST THE NEW, STRONGER case g: `libexec/
autoharn-service` and `serving/boundary_service.py` were temporarily reverted to their pre-round-1
content (`git show 3d2043e:...`, no `--pidfile` support at all) and driven through 15 trials of
SIX concurrent `autoharn service start` invocations each (higher concurrency than case d's two,
needed to make the inherently narrow timing window bite reliably) -- a direct process/`ss` census
right after each trial (the same shape case g now runs):

  trial 9: RACE SYMPTOM -- alive=[473685, 473688] pidfile_pid=473685

Two `serving.boundary_service` processes were simultaneously alive and matching the port's own
cmdline at once under the OLD code -- exactly the split-brain class SEVERE-2 named. The old code
and the temporary revert were then restored to this round's fixed versions.

A SECOND, GENUINE DEFECT surfaced by this round's OWN new code while developing the fix above,
also witnessed red before being fixed: the first draft of the structural fix set `SO_REUSEADDR`
on the manually-bound socket (to allow an honest quick restart after real traffic, since an
accepted connection's TIME_WAIT state otherwise blocks rebinding the same port for a while) --
but with `SO_REUSEADDR` set, Linux permits a SECOND socket to `bind()` the SAME address:port
successfully as long as NEITHER socket has called `listen()` yet, which uvicorn/asyncio only do
well after this process's own Python/FastAPI import overhead -- a wide-open window. Reproduction
(20 trials of six concurrent invocations against the `SO_REUSEADDR`-but-no-early-`listen()`
draft):

  g-race-structural-winner: FAIL -- pidfile names pid 477853, but the live/port-holding process
  is 477852

THE FIX: `listen()` is called synchronously immediately after `bind()`, in this same process,
before the pidfile is ever written -- the actual OS-level exclusivity boundary once
`SO_REUSEADDR` is set is `listen()`, not `bind()` alone; asyncio's own later `listen()` call on
the already-listening socket is a harmless no-op re-application of the same backlog. Post-fix:
25 trials of six concurrent invocations (race) plus an immediate restart-after-stop in the same
trial (to prove `SO_REUSEADDR`'s own restart benefit was not lost) -- zero race symptoms, zero
restart failures. The full nine-case fixture (a-i) below also passes reliably across many
consecutive runs.
